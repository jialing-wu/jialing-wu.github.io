What Is Network Centrality?

什么是网络中心性？

In any network, some people matter more than others. But "matter" can mean different things. Network centrality measures answer the question: who is important, and why?

在任何网络中，有些人比其他人更重要。但"重要"可以有不同的含义。网络中心性措施回答这个问题：谁很重要，为什么？

Think of a company office. The boss might be central because everyone reports to them (degree). A coordinator might be central because they connect different teams (betweenness). The best-connected person might be central because they can reach anyone quickly (closeness). A celebrity advisor might be central because they're trusted by important people (eigenvector). Same office, four different "centers."
想象一个公司办公室。老板可能很中心，因为每个人都向他们报告（度数）。协调员可能很中心，因为他们连接不同的团队（中介性）。最有联系的人可能很中心，因为他们可以快速到达任何人（亲密性）。一位有名的顾问可能很中心，因为他们被重要的人信任（特征向量）。同样的办公室，四个不同的"中心"。

The key insight: importance depends on the question you're asking. There's no single "most central" person—only the most central *for your goal*.

关键见解：重要性取决于你提的问题。没有单一的"最中心"的人——只有*对你的目标*最中心的人。

Four Types of Centrality

四种中心性类型

1. Degree Centrality: Popularity

1. 度数中心性：受欢迎程度

Definition: How many direct connections does a person have?

定义：一个人有多少直接连接？

Someone with many friends on social media has high degree centrality. They're popular, but that doesn't mean they're the best at bridging groups or reaching distant people.
在社交媒体上有许多朋友的人拥有高度数中心性。他们很受欢迎，但这并不意味着他们善于连接不同的群体或到达远处的人。
Use degree when: You want to find popular or highly-connected people. Examples: influencers on social media, hub airports in transportation networks, prolific authors in citation networks.
什么时候使用度数：你想找到受欢迎或高度连接的人。例子：社交媒体上的影响者、交通网络中的枢纽机场、引文网络中的多产作者。

2. Betweenness Centrality: Brokerage

2. 中介中心性：经纪作用

Definition: How often does a person lie on the shortest path between two others?

定义：一个人在其他两个人之间的最短路径上出现多少次？

Imagine two teams that don't talk to each other. The one person with connections to both is a broker—high betweenness. They control information flow. Remove them, and communication breaks down.
想象两个互不沟通的团队。与两个团队都有联系的那个人是经纪人——高中介性。他们控制信息流。移除他们，沟通就会中断。
Use betweenness when: You want to find connectors, brokers, or gatekeepers. Examples: organizational bridges, critical junctions in networks, people who control information flow.
什么时候使用中介性：你想找到连接者、经纪人或守门人。例子：组织中的桥梁、网络中的关键交汇点、控制信息流的人。

3. Closeness Centrality: Efficiency

3. 亲密中心性：效率

Definition: How quickly can a person reach everyone else in the network?

定义：一个人能有多快地到达网络中的所有其他人？

In a city, a person living downtown has high closeness—they can get anywhere fast. Someone in a remote suburb has low closeness—they need more time to reach most places.
在城市中，住在市中心的人拥有高亲密性——他们可以快速到达任何地方。住在偏远郊区的人拥有低亲密性——他们需要更多时间才能到达大多数地方。
Use closeness when: You care about speed, efficiency, or influence through rapid spread. Examples: optimal facility placement, pandemic modeling, information diffusion studies.
什么时候使用亲密性：你关心速度、效率或通过快速传播的影响力。例子：最优设施放置、疫情建模、信息传播研究。

4. Eigenvector Centrality: Prestige

4. 特征向量中心性：声望

Definition: Are you connected to people who are themselves well-connected? High eigenvector centrality means you're connected to "important" people.

定义：你是否与那些本身就是高度连接的人相连？高特征向量中心性意味着你与"重要"的人相连。

Having 10 friends isn't impressive if they're all nobodies. Having 2 friends who are celebrities is more impressive. Eigenvector centrality rewards connecting to *quality* connections, not just *quantity*.
如果10个朋友都是默默无闻的人，拥有10个朋友就不令人印象深刻。拥有2个名人朋友更令人印象深刻。特征向量中心性奖励与*高质量*的连接相连，而不仅仅是*数量*。
Use eigenvector when: Quality of connections matters, not just quantity. Examples: Google's PageRank algorithm, scientific prestige (cited by top researchers), social influence (followed by influential people).
什么时候使用特征向量：连接的质量很重要，而不仅仅是数量。例子：谷歌的PageRank算法、科学声望（被顶尖研究人员引用）、社会影响力（被有影响力的人关注）。

A Concrete Example

一个具体例子

Let's look at a small 5-person network: Alice, Bob, Carol, Dave, and Eve. Here are their connections:

让我们看一个小的5人网络：Alice、Bob、Carol、Dave和Eve。以下是他们的连接：

Connections:
  • Alice connects to: Bob, Carol
  • Bob connects to: Alice, Carol, Dave
  • Carol connects to: Alice, Bob, Eve
  • Dave connects to: Bob, Eve
  • Eve connects to: Carol, Dave
连接：
  • Alice 连接到：Bob、Carol
  • Bob 连接到：Alice、Carol、Dave
  • Carol 连接到：Alice、Bob、Eve
  • Dave 连接到：Bob、Eve
  • Eve 连接到：Carol、Dave

Degree: Who has the most friends?

度数：谁有最多朋友？

Rankings: Bob and Carol (3 connections each) > Alice, Dave, Eve (2 connections each)

排名：Bob 和 Carol（各 3 个连接）> Alice、Dave、Eve（各 2 个连接）

Bob and Carol are the most popular. They're hubs in this network.

Bob 和 Carol 最受欢迎。他们是这个网络中的枢纽。

Betweenness: Who's the best broker?

中介性：谁是最好的经纪人？

Rankings: Bob (highest) > Carol > Alice, Dave, Eve

排名：Bob（最高）> Carol > Alice、Dave、Eve

Bob sits between Alice and Dave—they must go through him to reach each other. He controls a critical connection. Carol bridges Alice/Bob to Eve, but less critically.

Bob 位于 Alice 和 Dave 之间——他们必须通过他才能到达彼此。他控制着一个关键连接。Carol 连接 Alice/Bob 到 Eve，但不太关键。

Closeness: Who's most central?

亲密性：谁最中心？

Rankings: Bob and Carol (highest) > Alice, Dave, Eve

排名：Bob 和 Carol（最高）> Alice、Dave、Eve

Bob and Carol can reach everyone in just 1–2 steps. Alice, Dave, and Eve need more hops. If information spreads from Bob or Carol, it reaches everyone fastest.

Bob 和 Carol 可以在 1-2 步内到达所有人。Alice、Dave 和 Eve 需要更多跳跃。如果信息从 Bob 或 Carol 传播，它会最快地到达所有人。

Eigenvector: Who's connected to important people?

特征向量：谁与重要的人相连？

Rankings: Carol and Bob (highest) > Alice, Dave, Eve

排名：Carol 和 Bob（最高）> Alice、Dave、Eve

Carol is connected to both Bob and Alice (both highly connected). Alice is only connected to Carol and Bob (both important). Dave and Eve are on the periphery. Quality of connections matters here.

Carol 与 Bob 和 Alice 相连（都是高度连接的）。Alice 只与 Carol 和 Bob 相连（都很重要）。Dave 和 Eve 在外围。这里连接的质量很重要。

Key takeaway: The "most central" person depends on what you care about. If you want to reach everyone fast, pick Bob or Carol. If you want information to spread through a broker, pick Bob. If you want prestige, pick Carol.

关键要点："最中心"的人取决于你关心什么。如果你想快速到达所有人，选择 Bob 或 Carol。如果你想让信息通过经纪人传播，选择 Bob。如果你想要声望，选择 Carol。

The Formulas

公式

Degree Centrality

度数中心性

$$C_D(v) = \deg(v)$$

Plain English: Count how many people this person is connected to. That's it. If Alice is connected to Bob and Carol, her degree centrality is 2.

简明英文：计算这个人有多少人相连。就是这样。如果 Alice 与 Bob 和 Carol 相连，她的度数中心性是 2。

Betweenness Centrality

中介中心性

$$C_B(v) = \sum_{s \neq v \neq t} \frac{\sigma_{st}(v)}{\sigma_{st}}$$

Plain English: For every pair of people (s and t), count how many of the shortest paths between them go through v. Add up all these counts. High betweenness means many shortest paths pass through you.

简明英文：对于每一对人（s 和 t），计算他们之间有多少条最短路径经过 v。将所有这些计数加起来。高中介性意味着许多最短路径通过你。

Closeness Centrality

亲密中心性

$$C_C(v) = \frac{1}{\sum_{u \neq v} d(v, u)}$$

Plain English: Calculate the shortest distance to every other person, then add all those distances. Take the inverse (divide 1 by that sum). Higher closeness means shorter distances on average.

简明英文：计算到所有其他人的最短距离，然后将所有这些距离相加。取倒数（用 1 除以该和）。更高的亲密性意味着平均距离更短。

Eigenvector Centrality

特征向量中心性

$$C_E(v) = \frac{1}{\lambda} \sum_{u \in N(v)} C_E(u)$$

Plain English: Your centrality is proportional to the centrality of people you're connected to. If you're connected to central people, you're more central. This creates a feedback loop that the algorithm solves iteratively. (Don't worry if this is abstract—R will calculate it for you!)

简明英文：你的中心性与你所连接的人的中心性成正比。如果你与中心人物相连，你就更中心。这创建了一个反馈循环，算法会迭代求解。（如果这有点抽象，不用担心——R 会为你计算！）

Computing Centrality in R

在 R 中计算中心性

We'll use the igraph package to compute all four measures. Here's a complete example:

我们将使用 igraph 包来计算所有四种度量。这是一个完整的例子：

Show R Code 显示 R 代码 
# Install igraph if you haven't already
# install.packages("igraph")

library(igraph)

# Create the example network
edges <- data.frame(
  from = c("Alice", "Alice", "Bob", "Bob", "Bob", "Carol", "Carol", "Carol", "Dave", "Eve"),
  to   = c("Bob", "Carol", "Alice", "Carol", "Dave", "Alice", "Bob", "Eve", "Bob", "Carol")
)

# Create an undirected graph
g <- graph_from_data_frame(edges, directed = FALSE)

# Compute all four centrality measures
degree_cent <- degree(g, mode = "all")
between_cent <- betweenness(g)
close_cent <- closeness(g)
eigen_cent <- eigen_centrality(g)$vector

# Combine into a table
results <- data.frame(
  Person = names(degree_cent),
  Degree = round(degree_cent, 3),
  Betweenness = round(between_cent, 3),
  Closeness = round(close_cent, 3),
  Eigenvector = round(eigen_cent, 3)
)

print(results)

# Visualize the network with node sizes based on degree
plot(g, 
     vertex.size = degree_cent * 15,
     vertex.label.cex = 1.2,
     vertex.label.dist = 0.5,
     edge.width = 2,
     main = "Network Centrality Example")

What this code does:

这段代码的作用：

  • Creates the example network with 5 people and 10 connections
  • 创建包含 5 个人和 10 个连接的示例网络
  • Computes degree, betweenness, closeness, and eigenvector centrality for each person
  • 计算每个人的度数、中介性、亲密性和特征向量中心性
  • Displays results in a clean table
  • 在干净的表格中显示结果
  • Visualizes the network with node sizes proportional to degree
  • 将网络可视化，节点大小与度数成正比

Summary: Which Centrality for Which Question?

总结：哪个问题用哪个中心性？

Question 问题 Use This Measure 使用此度量
Who is most popular? 谁最受欢迎？ Degree 度数
Who controls information flow? 谁控制信息流？ Betweenness 中介性
Who can reach everyone fastest? 谁能最快地到达所有人？ Closeness 亲密性
Who is connected to important people? 谁与重要的人相连？ Eigenvector 特征向量

Remember: Network centrality is a tool, not a truth. Different measures answer different questions. Always ask yourself: "What am I trying to find?" before choosing a centrality measure. The answer will guide you.

记住：网络中心性是一个工具，而不是真理。不同的度量回答不同的问题。在选择中心性度量之前，始终问自己："我想找什么？"答案会指导你。
