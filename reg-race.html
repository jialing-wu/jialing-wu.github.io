What Is This?

Race and ethnicity are among the most consequential variables in social science — and among the most misused. This page addresses how race and ethnicity are defined, measured, and analyzed in quantitative research, and how researchers can avoid common methodological pitfalls.

Defining and Coding Race: Categories, History, and Critique

定义和编码种族：类别、历史与批判

The problem: You're building a dataset and need to include race or ethnicity. Should you use categories from the U.S. Census? Create your own categories? What if your respondents selected multiple categories? The answers depend on understanding that race is not a fixed biological fact, but a social and political construction that varies across countries and changes over time.

问题：你在构建数据集，需要包含种族或族裔。你应该使用美国人口普查的类别吗？创建自己的类别？如果受访者选择多个类别怎么办？答案取决于认识到种族不是固定的生物学事实，而是因国家而异、随时间变化的社会和政治建构。

Race as Social Construction, Not Biology

种族作为社会建构，而非生物学

Race categories are social and political creations, not biological facts. The same person might be classified as "colored" under apartheid South Africa, "coloured" in post-apartheid South Africa (a different category), and "mixed" in the contemporary United States. Brazil uses neither the black-white binary nor the U.S. Census categories. Census categories themselves have changed: "mulatto" appeared on the 1890 U.S. Census but not again until 2000. The consequences are not abstract — legal rights, welfare benefits, and voter redistricting all depend on these definitions. When you code race, you're encoding political history into your data.

种族类别是社会和政治创造，不是生物学事实。同一个人在南非种族隔离制度下可能被分类为"colored"，在后种族隔离时期被分类为"coloured"（不同的类别），在当代美国被分类为"混血"。巴西既不使用黑白二元论，也不使用美国人口普查的类别。人口普查类别本身已经改变：1890年美国人口普查中出现了"混血"(mulatto)，但之后直到2000年才再次出现。这些后果不是抽象的——法律权利、福利福利和选民重新分配都取决于这些定义。当你编码种族时，你就是在把政治历史编入你的数据。

Analogy: Categorizing music into genres. "Rock" covers everything from Elvis to death metal—it tells you something about era and instrumentation, but obscures more than it reveals. Similarly, "Hispanic" covers Mexican-Americans, Puerto Ricans, Cuban-Americans, and Central American immigrants with radically different political histories, economic trajectories, and policy interests. The category is administratively useful but scientifically crude.
类比：将音乐分类为流派。"摇滚乐"涵盖从埃尔维斯到死亡金属的一切——它告诉你一些关于时代和乐器的信息，但隐蔽的比揭示的更多。类似地，"西班牙裔"涵盖墨西哥裔美国人、波多黎各人、古巴裔美国人和中美洲移民，他们的政治历史、经济轨迹和政策利益差异巨大。该类别在行政上很有用，但在科学上很粗糙。
When to use / not use: Use nominal coding (separate dummy variables) almost always for race/ethnicity. Never use ordinal coding (treating race as 1=White, 2=Black, 3=Asian, 4=Hispanic) because this implies a ranking that is both scientifically indefensible and ethically offensive. When you have multiple responses, preserve them or create interaction terms—do not collapse "Asian-White" and "Black-White" into a single "multiracial" category. Don't use these categories when: you're testing biological hypotheses (you're really measuring ancestry, which requires genetic data); you lack theoretical justification for why these particular categories matter for your outcome.
何时用/不用：对种族/族裔，几乎总是使用标称编码（单独的虚拟变量）。永远不要使用序数编码（将种族视为1=白人，2=黑人，3=亚洲人，4=西班牙裔），因为这意味着一种排名，既在科学上无法辩护，在伦理上也令人反感。当你有多个回答时，保留它们或创建交互项——不要将"亚洲人-白人"和"黑人-白人"折叠成单一的"多种族"类别。不要在以下情况使用这些类别：你在测试生物学假说（你真正测量的是祖先，需要遗传数据）；你缺乏理论依据来解释为什么这些特定类别对你的结果很重要。
Social science example: Racial health disparities. Research on why Black Americans have higher rates of maternal mortality, hypertension, and COVID-19 mortality finds dramatically different results depending on how race was measured. Studies using self-reported race vs. observer-classified race (e.g., clinician assessment) vs. ancestry reveal different biological and social mechanisms. Self-report captures social identity and discrimination exposure; observer-classification captures stereotype and bias; ancestry reveals genetic variation. Each answers a different question. Conflating them produces confusion about whether disparities are due to genetic differences (which are small), discrimination and stress (the primary mechanism), or healthcare access.
社会科学例子：种族健康差异。关于为什么黑人美国人患产妇死亡率、高血压和COVID-19死亡率更高的研究发现，根据种族的测量方式，结果差异很大。使用自我报告种族与观察者分类种族（例如，临床医生评估）与祖先的研究显示不同的生物学和社会机制。自我报告捕捉社会身份和歧视暴露；观察者分类捕捉刻板印象和偏见；祖先揭示遗传变异。每一个都回答了不同的问题。混淆它们会导致关于差异是由于遗传差异（很小）、歧视和压力（主要机制）还是医疗保健获取的混淆。

Coding Approaches: Dummies, Aggregation, and Heterogeneity

编码方法：虚拟变量、聚合与异质性

Use separate dummy variables for each racial/ethnic category. This avoids implying an ordering (as ordinal coding does) and lets you estimate group-specific effects. The reference category matters: if you use White as the reference, you estimate the effect of being another race relative to White. If you use a non-dominant group as reference, your results look different but contain the same information. Aggregation is tempting but dangerous. "Asian American" is a Census category that aggregates Chinese, Vietnamese, Filipino, Korean, Indian, and Japanese Americans—groups with different median incomes, political alignment, immigration dates, and immigration legal status. Lumping them together erases this heterogeneity. Similarly, "Hispanic" masks the distinction between Puerto Rican (U.S. citizens by birth), undocumented Mexican immigrants, and Cuban-American middle-class professionals. If your dataset has sufficient sample size, disaggregate.

为每个种族/族裔类别使用单独的虚拟变量。这避免了暗示排序（序数编码所做的），让你估计特定群体的效果。参考类别很重要：如果你使用白人作为参考，你估计的是属于另一种种族相对于白人的效果。如果你使用非主导群体作为参考，你的结果看起来不同，但包含相同的信息。聚合很诱人，但很危险。"亚洲人美国人"是一个人口普查类别，汇总了中国人、越南人、菲律宾人、韩国人、印度人和日本人美国人——这些群体有不同的中位收入、政治立场、移民日期和移民法律地位。将它们合并在一起会消除这种异质性。类似地，"西班牙裔"掩盖了波多黎各人（出生时的美国公民）、无证件的墨西哥移民和古巴裔美国中产阶级专业人士之间的区别。如果你的数据集有足够的样本量，请分解。

Analogy: You notice restaurants in your city cluster spatially. You code location as "downtown" vs. "suburban" and find suburban restaurants have lower prices. But maybe what matters is that downtown has expensive boutique restaurants while the suburbs have family chains and cheap tacos. Aggregating masks heterogeneity that explains your outcome. Same with race — aggregating masks the within-group variation that often exceeds between-group variation.
类比：你注意到你城市的餐厅在空间上聚集。你将位置编码为"市中心"与"郊区"，发现郊区餐厅价格更低。但也许重要的是市中心有昂贵的精品餐厅，而郊区有家庭连锁店和便宜的玉米卷。聚合掩盖了解释你的结果的群体内差异。种族也是一样——聚合掩盖了群体内差异，这种差异通常超过群体间差异。
When to use / not use: Disaggregate when: you have sufficient sample size in each subgroup (generally n>30 minimum, n>100 preferred); your theory specifies why different subgroups should differ; you're interested in subgroup heterogeneity. Aggregate when: your primary goal is comparing minorities to a dominant group and subgroup differences are not theoretically important; your sample size is small enough that disaggregation produces unreliable estimates (very wide confidence intervals).
何时用/不用：分解当：你在每个子群中有足够的样本量（通常最少n>30，更喜欢n>100）；你的理论指定了不同子群应该不同的原因；你对子群异质性感兴趣。聚合当：你的主要目标是将少数族裔与主导群体进行比较，子群差异在理论上不重要；你的样本量足够小，分解会产生不可靠的估计（非常宽的置信区间）。
Social science example: Research on voting behavior aggregates all "minorities" against "whites" or distinguishes only Black, Hispanic, and Asian voters. But aggregating Asian American voters masks that Vietnamese and Korean Americans vote quite differently from Chinese and Indian Americans on many issues—partly due to Cold War immigration patterns and partly due to different class positions. Studies that disaggregate find that treating Asian Americans as a monolith predicts voting behavior poorly and obscures the diverse coalition-building opportunities in electoral politics.
社会科学例子：关于投票行为的研究将所有"少数族裔"聚合在对抗"白人"的立场上，或仅区分黑人、西班牙裔和亚洲人选民。但聚合亚洲人美国人选民掩盖了越南裔和韩国裔美国人在许多问题上的投票方式与中国和印度人美国人相当不同——部分是由于冷战移民模式，部分是由于不同的阶级地位。分解的研究发现，将亚洲人美国人视为一个整体对投票行为的预测很差，并掩盖了选举政治中多样化的联盟建设机会。

Measurement Approaches: Self-Report, Observer-Classification, and Ancestry

测量方法：自我报告、观察者分类与祖先

Your measurement method shapes what you're actually measuring. Self-reported race captures how people identify and often correlates with discrimination exposure and community belonging. Observer-classified race (e.g., a clinician noting patient race, or a researcher coding race from photographs) captures what others perceive and expect, often mediated by stereotype and bias. Ancestry (from genetic data, genealogy records, or surname analysis) captures biological lineage but is often less relevant to social outcomes than racial identity. Each has validity for different questions. If you're studying discrimination, self-report reveals identity-based discrimination; observer-classification reveals perceptual bias. If you're studying health disparities driven by social stress and healthcare access, self-report is more relevant. Genetic ancestry matters for population genetics, not usually for social science. The key mistake: using observer-classification to answer questions about identity, or vice versa.

你的测量方法塑造了你实际测量的内容。自我报告的种族捕捉人们的身份认同，通常与歧视暴露和社区归属感相关。观察者分类的种族（例如，临床医生记录患者种族，或研究人员从照片中编码种族）捕捉他人的认知和期望，通常由刻板印象和偏见调解。祖先（来自遗传数据、系谱记录或姓氏分析）捕捉生物血统，但通常与社会成果的相关性不如种族身份。每一个对不同的问题都有效。如果你在研究歧视，自我报告揭示了基于身份的歧视；观察者分类揭示了感知偏见。如果你在研究由社会压力和医疗保健获取驱动的健康差异，自我报告更相关。遗传祖先对群体遗传学很重要，对社会科学通常不重要。关键错误：使用观察者分类来回答关于身份的问题，或反之。

Analogy: Measuring wealth through self-reported income vs. asset ownership vs. tax records. Each captures different information. Self-report reveals what people think they earn and may include informal income; assets reveal long-term accumulation and credit access; tax records show documented income and legal status. Using one to answer questions designed for another produces confusion.
类比：通过自我报告收入对财富进行测量，与资产所有权对比，与税务记录对比。每一个都捕捉不同的信息。自我报告揭示了人们认为他们赚多少，可能包括非正式收入；资产揭示了长期积累和信用获取；税务记录显示了记录在案的收入和法律地位。使用一个来回答为另一个设计的问题会产生混淆。
When to use / not use: Use self-report when: studying identity, belonging, or discrimination; asking questions where what people believe about themselves matters; administering your own surveys. Use observer-classification when: studying implicit bias or perceptual discrimination; using secondary data where only observer race is recorded. Use ancestry when: population genetics is your goal; you have genetic data available. Avoid using observer-classification to proxy for self-identified race or vice versa—they measure different constructs.
何时用/不用：自我报告使用：研究身份、归属或歧视；提出其中人们对自己的看法很重要的问题；进行自己的调查。观察者分类使用：研究隐性偏见或感知歧视；使用仅记录观察者种族的二手数据。祖先使用：群体遗传学是你的目标；你有可用的遗传数据。避免使用观察者分类作为自认种族的代理，反之亦然——它们测量不同的构造。
Social science example: Healthcare disparities research increasingly distinguishes between self-reported race and clinician-assessed race. Studies find that Black patients whose race was misclassified (clinicians recorded them as white) still experienced health disparities similar to correctly identified Black patients—suggesting that the social experience and discrimination that produces disparities operates through self-identified race and community identity, not through what clinicians think. This insight redirects attention from medical providers' awareness of race to the lived experience of being racialized.
社会科学例子：医疗保健差异研究越来越多地区分自我报告的种族和临床医生评估的种族。研究发现，种族被错误分类的黑人患者（临床医生将其记录为白人）仍然经历了与正确识别的黑人患者类似的健康差异——这表明产生差异的社会经验和歧视通过自我认同的种族和社区身份运作，而不是通过临床医生的想法。这一见解将注意力从医疗提供者对种族的认识转向被种族化的生活经验。

Analyzing Disparities: Causation, Mediation, and Intersectionality

分析差距：因果关系、中介与交叉性

The problem: You observe that racial group A has worse health outcomes than group B. What explains this gap? Is it discrimination? Genetics? Behavior? Income? If you "control for income," do you reveal the true effect of race, or do you accidentally remove the mechanism (income discrimination) that produces the gap? When you disaggregate by gender, should you add gender and race effects, or multiply them?

问题：你观察到种族群体A的健康结果比群体B更糟。什么解释了这种差距？是歧视吗？遗传学？行为？收入？如果你"控制收入"，你是揭示了种族的真实影响，还是不小心移除了产生差距的机制（收入歧视）？当你按性别分解时，你应该添加性别和种族的效果，还是相乘？

The "Race as a Cause" Debate: What Can We Estimate?

"种族作为原因"辩论：我们能估计什么？

Race cannot be randomly assigned in an experiment. This poses a fundamental problem for causal inference: without randomization, we must make strong assumptions about unmeasured confounders. Statistician Holland argued we cannot estimate causal effects of race because we cannot experimentally intervene on race. Sociologist Kohler-Hausmann countered that the question is poorly framed: we can estimate causal effects of racial discrimination (a policy or practice) or the effect of being perceived as a particular race (a mechanism), but not the effect of "being Black" in some abstract sense. The practical implication: your causal estimates from observational data are only as good as your assumptions about confounding. If you believe unobserved variables (like parental expectations shaped by racial socialization) influence both race and outcomes, your estimates of "race effects" will be biased. The solution is not to avoid studying race — it's to be precise about what causal quantity you're estimating.

种族不能在实验中被随机分配。这对因果推断提出了一个基本问题：没有随机化，我们必须对未测量的混淆因子做出强烈的假设。统计学家Holland辩称，我们不能估计种族的因果效应，因为我们不能在种族上进行实验干预。社会学家Kohler-Hausmann反驳说，问题的框架不当：我们可以估计种族歧视（政策或实践）的因果效应，或被认知为特定种族的影响（一种机制），但不是"作为黑人"在某种抽象意义上的效应。实践含义：你从观察数据中的因果估计只与你关于混淆的假设一样好。如果你相信未观察到的变量（如由种族社会化形成的父母期望）同时影响种族和结果，你对"种族效应"的估计将会有偏差。解决方案不是避免研究种族——而是精确了解你估计的因果数量。

Analogy: Estimating the effect of "being male" on earnings faces the same problem: maleness is not exogenous. Men receive different socialization, occupational guidance, and workplace treatment. We can estimate the effect of discrimination based on perceived gender (experimentally manipulable through name-swapping on resumes), or the effect of specific male-typical occupations (educational choice is partially endogenous), but not "maleness per se." This reframing makes the causal problem tractable.
类比：估计"作为男性"对收入的影响面临同样的问题：男性不是外生的。男性接受不同的社会化、职业指导和工作场所待遇。我们可以估计基于感知性别的歧视的影响（通过简历上的名字交换在实验上可操纵），或特定男性典型职业的影响（教育选择在部分上是内生的），但不是"男性本身"。这种重新框架使因果问题变得容易处理。
When to use / not use: Estimate the effect of race as exposure to discrimination (mechanisms, policies, or perceptions that are observable and vary). Don't estimate the effect of "being a particular race" as if it's an exogenous treatment. Make explicit what confounders you're assuming away (parental education, neighborhood effects, genetic ancestry—whatever you believe is not causally driven by race). Sensitivity analyses showing how results change under different confounder assumptions are essential.
何时用/不用：估计种族作为歧视暴露的影响（机制、政策或可观察且变化的认知）。不要估计"作为特定种族"的影响，就好像它是外生处理一样。明确说明你假设的混淆因子（父母教育、邻域效应、遗传祖先——无论你认为什么不是由种族因果驱动的）。显示结果在不同混淆因子假设下如何变化的敏感性分析是必需的。
Social science example: A study finds that Black workers earn 10% less than white workers with the same education and experience. Is this the causal effect of race? It depends on unmeasured confounders: workplace quality, occupational segregation, discrimination in hiring and promotion, social networks, and accumulated advantages from prior generations. Studies using audit experiments (sending identical resumes with Black and white names) find 25-50% callback rate differences, directly estimating discrimination as a mechanism. The observational 10% gap likely includes both discrimination and other factors; the experimental gap isolates discrimination. Different quantities answer different questions.
社会科学例子：一项研究发现，具有相同教育和经验的黑人工人的收入比白人工人少10%。这是种族的因果效应吗？这取决于未测量的混淆因子：工作场所质量、职业隔离、招聘和晋升中的歧视、社交网络以及来自前代的累积优势。使用审计实验（发送具有黑人和白人名字的相同简历）的研究发现回调率差异为25-50%，直接估计歧视作为一种机制。观察到的10%差距可能既包括歧视，也包括其他因素；实验差距隔离了歧视。不同的数量回答不同的问题。

Mediators vs. Confounders: Don't Accidentally Remove the Mechanism

中介因子与混淆因子：不要不小心移除机制

If income mediates the race-health relationship (race → lower income → worse health), controlling for income removes the mechanism you're trying to measure. The racial gap in health is partly caused by lower incomes; if you remove income from the regression, you underestimate the total effect of race. Conversely, neighborhood quality might be a confounder: racial segregation is partly a legacy of discriminatory housing policy, but neighborhood crime and school quality are not mechanisms of race itself—they're correlates. Distinguishing mediators from confounders requires theory, not just statistics. A variable is a mediator if race causally affects it, which then affects the outcome. A variable is a confounder if it affects both race and the outcome, and race doesn't cause it. In practice: parental education is a confounder (race doesn't cause your parents' education, but your parents' race might correlate with theirs); occupational choice is a mediator (racial discrimination can push people into different occupations). The same variable can be both depending on your causal model—which is why explicitly stating your assumptions matters.

如果收入调解种族-健康关系（种族→较低收入→更差的健康），控制收入会移除你试图测量的机制。健康中的种族差距部分是由较低收入造成的；如果你从回归中移除收入，你会低估种族的总体影响。相反，邻近社区质量可能是一个混淆因子：种族隔离部分是歧视性住房政策的遗产，但邻近社区犯罪和学校质量不是种族本身的机制——它们是相关因素。区分中介因子和混淆因子需要理论，而不仅仅是统计学。如果种族因果影响变量，然后影响结果，变量是中介因子。如果变量影响种族和结果，而种族不会导致它，变量是混淆因子。实际上：父母教育是混淆因子（种族不会导致你父母的教育，但你父母的种族可能与他们的教育相关）；职业选择是中介因子（种族歧视会将人推向不同的职业）。同一变量可能既是，取决于你的因果模型——这就是为什么明确说明你的假设很重要。

Analogy: The gender-wage gap in economics. Men earn more. If you control for field (more men in higher-paid fields), you estimate discrimination within field. If you don't control for field, you estimate the total effect of gender (including occupational segregation due to discrimination). The gap due to occupational segregation is not a confounder to remove—it's part of the causal mechanism of gender-based discrimination. Removing it understates the problem.
类比：经济学中的性别薪酬差距。男性收入更多。如果你控制领域（更多男性在薪酬较高的领域），你估计的是领域内的歧视。如果你不控制领域，你估计的是性别的总体影响（包括由于歧视导致的职业隔离）。由职业隔离造成的差距不是要移除的混淆因子——它是基于性别的歧视的因果机制的一部分。移除它会低估问题。
When to use / not use: Control for confounders to isolate the effect of race net of other causes. Don't control for mediators if you want the total effect of race (including its effects through mechanisms like income discrimination). Consider separate regression models: (1) race predicting outcome (total effect); (2) race predicting mediator; (3) both race and mediator predicting outcome. Model 3 minus models 1 and 2 gives direct and indirect effects. Causal diagrams (DAGs) help you visualize which variables to control.
何时用/不用：控制混淆因子以隔离种族的效应，排除其他原因。如果你想要种族的总体影响（包括其通过收入歧视等机制的影响），不要控制中介因子。考虑分别的回归模型：(1)种族预测结果（总体效应）；(2)种族预测中介因子；(3)种族和中介因子都预测结果。模型3减去模型1和2给出直接和间接效应。因果图（DAG）帮助你可视化要控制哪些变量。
Social science example: Racial disparities in traffic stops. One study found Black drivers are pulled over more frequently. If you control for neighborhood (more Black drivers in high-crime neighborhoods, more police presence), do you remove a confounder or the mechanism? It depends: if racial segregation is itself a legacy of discriminatory housing policy, then neighborhood is part of the mechanism of racial injustice. Controlling for it obscures the full extent of racial disparities in policing. But if you're specifically studying policing bias conditional on neighborhood, then neighborhood is legitimately a control. The answer depends on your research question and causal assumptions.
社会科学例子：交通停止中的种族差异。一项研究发现黑人驾驶员被拦截的频率更高。如果你控制邻域（更多黑人驾驶员在高犯罪率邻域，更多警察存在），你是移除混淆因子还是机制？这取决于：如果种族隔离本身是歧视性住房政策的遗产，那么邻域是种族不公正机制的一部分。控制它会掩盖警务中种族差异的全部程度。但如果你特别在研究以邻域为条件的警务偏见，那么邻域合法地是一个控制。答案取决于你的研究问题和因果假设。

Intersectionality: Multiplicative Effects, Not Additive

交叉性：乘法效应，而非加法

Intersectionality in quantitative analysis means that the effect of race is not the same for everyone—it varies by gender, class, and other social positions. The effect of being a Black woman is not the sum of the effect of being Black plus the effect of being a woman. It's often multiplicative and frequently larger than either main effect alone. Simple robustness checks: estimate your model separately for men and women, and separately for different income groups. If coefficients differ substantially, you likely have intersectional effects. Formal methods: use interaction terms (Race × Gender) in regression. Be careful with interpretation: the interaction coefficient tells you how much the race effect differs between men and women, not the total effect for a particular group. Marginal effects (computing predicted values for each group) are more interpretable. Hierarchical models allow random intercepts and slopes by group. The harder question: does your sample include enough Black women, or low-income Asian Americans, to estimate intersectional effects precisely? Small subgroup sizes produce large confidence intervals.

量化分析中的交叉性意味着种族的影响对每个人都不相同——它因性别、阶级和其他社会地位而异。作为黑人女性的效果不是作为黑人的效果加上作为女性的效果之和。它通常是乘法的，通常大于单独的任何主体效应。简单的稳健性检查：为男性和女性分别估计你的模型，为不同的收入群体分别估计。如果系数差异很大，你可能有交叉性效应。正式方法：在回归中使用交互项（种族×性别）。小心解释：交互系数告诉你种族效应在男性和女性之间相差多少，而不是特定群体的总体影响。边际效应（为每个群体计算预测值）更易于解释。分层模型允许按群体的随机截距和斜率。更难的问题：你的样本是否包含足够的黑人女性或低收入亚洲人美国人来精确估计交叉性效应？小子群大小会产生大的置信区间。

Analogy: Medication effects often interact. A drug might work well for men but poorly for women; it might work at normal doses but cause overdose effects at higher doses. The effect isn't additive—it's context-dependent. Social inequality works similarly: discrimination and advantage are experienced differently depending on your intersecting identities.
类比：药物效应经常相互作用。一种药物可能对男性效果很好，但对女性效果不佳；它可能在正常剂量下起作用，但在更高剂量下会导致过量效应。效果不是加法的——它是上下文相关的。社会不平等的运作方式类似：歧视和优势因你的交叉身份而异。
When to use / not use: Always check for intersectional effects—at minimum, stratify analyses by major categories (gender, class, region). Use interaction terms to formally test for differences. Don't assume race effects are constant across all demographic groups. Don't interpret main effects for race as if they apply universally. Avoid overfitting by testing too many interactions if your sample is small. Consider pre-registering which interactions you'll test to avoid false positives from fishing.
何时用/不用：始终检查交叉性效应——至少，按主要类别（性别、阶级、地区）分层分析。使用交互项正式测试差异。不要假设种族效应对所有人口群体都是常数。不要解释种族的主体效应，就好像它们普遍适用。通过在样本小时测试太多交互来避免过度拟合。考虑预注册你将测试的交互以避免从钓鱼导致的假阳性。
Social science example: Criminal justice research on racial disparities in sentencing. If you run a simple regression of race on sentence length, you find substantial racial gaps. But gender matters: Black men receive longer sentences than white men, but the racial gap for women is smaller. Class matters: low-income defendants receive harsher sentences, and this compounds for low-income minorities. Age matters: younger defendants of all races receive harsher sentences partly because they're perceived as more dangerous (a stereotype that disproportionately affects minorities). Intersectional analysis reveals that the impact of being Black in criminal court is not uniform—it depends on whether you're a man or woman, young or old, poor or middle-class.
社会科学例子：关于量刑中种族差异的刑事司法研究。如果你运行种族对判决长度的简单回归，你会发现大量的种族差距。但性别很重要：黑人男性比白人男性获得更长的判决，但女性的种族差距更小。阶级很重要：低收入被告获得更严厉的判决，这对低收入少数族裔来说更加复杂。年龄很重要：所有种族的年轻被告获得更严厉的判决，部分是因为他们被视为更危险（一种刻板印象，对少数族裔的影响尤为不利）。交叉性分析揭示了在刑事法庭上作为黑人的影响并不一致——这取决于你是男性还是女性、年轻还是年老、贫穷还是中产阶级。

Distinguishing Racial Gaps from Racial Effects

区分种族差距与种族效应

A racial gap is descriptive: group A has outcome X, group B has outcome Y, therefore A - B is a gap. No causal claim. A racial effect is causal: being race A caused outcome X. Computing a racial gap is straightforward; estimating a racial effect is hard (it requires causal assumptions). Many studies conflate these. For example, "there's a 20% wage gap between white and Black workers" is a gap—purely descriptive. Whether this gap is caused by discrimination, differences in education, differences in experience, employer stereotypes, occupational segregation, or some combination is a causal question requiring modeling. A gap persists even after controlling for observed characteristics; an effect requires assumptions about unmeasured confounding. The most honest approach: report both the unadjusted gap (descriptive) and the adjusted gap (after controlling for measured confounders), and acknowledge the causal ambiguity.

种族差距是描述性的：群体A有结果X，群体B有结果Y，因此A - B是一个差距。没有因果主张。种族效应是因果的：作为种族A导致了结果X。计算种族差距很简单；估计种族效应很难（它需要因果假设）。许多研究混淆了这些。例如，"白人和黑人工人之间存在20%的薪酬差距"是一个差距——纯粹是描述性的。这种差距是否由歧视、教育差异、经验差异、雇主刻板印象、职业隔离或某种组合引起，是一个需要建模的因果问题。即使在控制观察特征后，差距仍然存在；效应需要关于未测量混淆的假设。最诚实的方法：报告未调整的差距（描述性）和调整的差距（控制测量的混淆因子后），并承认因果歧义。

Analogy: Height differs between men and women (a gap). This gap is partly genetic, partly nutritional, partly historical (women were more poorly nourished in past generations). You can measure the gap easily; you can't estimate how much of it is caused by each factor without additional data and assumptions.
类比：身高因性别而异（一个差距）。这个差距部分是遗传的，部分是营养的，部分是历史的（妇女在过去的几代中营养不良）。你可以轻松测量差距；没有额外的数据和假设，你无法估计其中多少由每个因素引起。
When to use / not use: Report gaps when: you want to describe current inequalities; you're documenting disparities for policy purposes. Estimate effects when: you have theory about mechanisms; you're testing whether a specific factor explains the gap; you've made explicit causal assumptions. Don't claim to estimate effects without justifying your causal model. Don't confuse persistent gaps with evidence of discrimination—gaps can persist due to unmeasured factors.
何时用/不用：报告差距当：你想描述当前的不平等；你在为政策目的记录差异。估计效应当：你有关于机制的理论；你在测试特定因素是否解释了差距；你已经明确了因果假设。不要声称估计效应而不证明你的因果模型。不要将持久差距与歧视证据混淆——差距可能由于未测量因素而持续。
Social science example: The "achievement gap" in education—the gap in test scores between white and Black students. This gap is real and documented. Whether it's caused by discrimination in school funding, stereotype threat, differences in parental education, differences in early childhood experiences, residential segregation, genetic differences (which the evidence does not support), or some combination is contested. Better framing: call it the "opportunity gap" and focus on schools, teaching quality, and resources as mechanisms rather than assuming the gap reflects student characteristics.
社会科学例子：教育中的"成就差距"——白人和黑人学生之间的测试分数差距。这个差距是真实的、有据可查的。是由学校资金中的歧视、刻板印象威胁、父母教育的差异、早期儿童经历的差异、住宅隔离、遗传差异（证据不支持这一点）还是某种组合引起的，是有争议的。更好的框架：称其为"机会差距"，并将学校、教学质量和资源作为机制重点，而不是假设差距反映了学生特征。

Measurement Bias: Testing Data and Measures Across Racial Groups

测量偏差：测试种族群体间的数据和测量

The problem: You've coded race in your data, or you're using a dataset where race is already coded. But what if the same survey question means different things to different racial groups? What if your measure of "conservatism" is actually measuring stereotype-driven responses for minorities but ideological content for majorities? What if administrative data (police stops, hospitalizations, arrests) reflects enforcement patterns, not actual rates? You need to test whether your measurement is valid and unbiased across groups.

问题：你已经在数据中编码了种族，或者你在使用一个已经编码了种族的数据集。但如果同一个调查问题对不同的种族群体意思不同呢？如果你对"保守主义"的测量实际上是对少数族裔的刻板印象驱动反应的测量，但对多数族裔的意识形态内容的测量呢？如果行政数据（警察拦截、住院、逮捕）反映执法模式，而不是实际率率呢？你需要测试你的测量在各群体中是否有效和无偏。

Measurement Invariance: Does the Scale Mean the Same Thing Across Groups?

测量不变性：量表在群体间的含义是否相同？

Measurement invariance tests whether the same survey item or scale means the same thing across racial groups. Three levels: configural (the factor structure is the same), metric (the loadings are the same), and scalar (the intercepts are the same). A test might measure "political knowledge" for white respondents but "test-taking skill" for minorities. Test this by fitting a confirmatory factor analysis (CFA) model separately by race, then constraining parameters across groups and comparing fit. If metric invariance fails (loadings differ), the scale doesn't measure the same construct. If scalar invariance fails (intercepts differ), group comparisons are biased even if the construct is measured. For example, research on the IAT (Implicit Association Test) finds that it measures test-taking skill and prior exposure to the test more than it measures implicit bias—and this varies by race. Administrative data has a particular invariance problem: police stop data doesn't measure crime rates, it measures police behavior. Criminal records don't measure guilt, they measure who got arrested, tried, and convicted (filtered by wealth, legal representation, and prosecutor bias).

测量不变性测试同一问卷项目或量表在种族群体间是否意思相同。三个级别：配置（因子结构相同）、公制（负载相同）和标量（截距相同）。对白人受访者来说，测试可能测量"政治知识"，对少数族裔来说是"应试技能"。通过按种族分别拟合确认因子分析（CFA）模型，然后跨群体约束参数并比较拟合来测试这一点。如果公制不变性失败（负载不同），量表不会测量相同的构造。如果标量不变性失败（截距不同），即使测量构造也会有偏差的群体比较。例如，关于IAT（隐式联想测试）的研究发现，它更多地测量应试技能和之前对测试的暴露，而不是隐含偏见——这因种族而异。行政数据有特定的不变性问题：警察停止数据不测量犯罪率，它测量警察行为。犯罪记录不测量内疚，它测量谁被逮捕、审判和定罪（由财富、法律代理和检察官偏见过滤）。

Analogy: A bathroom scale that measures weight accurately for light people but adds 10 pounds for heavy people. You can compare two light people's weights, but cross-group comparisons are biased. The scale "works" within groups but is non-invariant across groups. This is measurement bias.
类比：一个浴室秤，对轻的人准确测量体重，但对重的人增加10磅。你可以比较两个轻人的体重，但跨群体比较有偏差。该秤在群体内"起作用"，但在群体间是不变的。这是测量偏差。
When to use / not use: Test for measurement invariance before using the same scale to compare racial groups. Use multi-group CFA. If metric or scalar invariance fails, estimate group-specific parameters or create different scales. Don't assume standard scales are unbiased across groups—many psychological and sociological measures were developed on white samples and may not be invariant. Always report invariance testing as part of your methods.
何时用/不用：在使用相同的量表比较种族群体之前测试测量不变性。使用多群CFA。如果公制或标量不变性失败，估计特定群体的参数或创建不同的量表。不要假设标准量表在各群体间是无偏的——许多心理学和社会学测量是在白人样本上开发的，可能不是不变的。始终将不变性测试报告为你的方法的一部分。
Social science example: Research on "racial resentment" using the standard Kinder-Sears scale finds that Black and white respondents interpret the same items differently. A question like "If Black people don't get ahead, it's because they don't work hard enough" measures racial stereotype for Black respondents but ideological agreement for white respondents. Studies testing scalar invariance find that the scale is not invariant across race, making simple comparisons misleading. Correcting for this changes substantive conclusions about racial attitudes.
社会科学例子：使用标准Kinder-Sears量表进行"种族怨恨"研究发现，黑人和白人受访者对相同项目的解释不同。一个问题，如"如果黑人不能成功，那是因为他们不够努力"，对黑人受访者来说测量种族刻板印象，但对白人受访者来说是意识形态同意。测试标量不变性的研究发现，该量表在种族间不是不变的，使简单比较具有欺骗性。更正这一点会改变关于种族态度的实质性结论。

Differential Item Functioning (DIF) and Psychometric Bias

差分项目功能（DIF）与心理测量偏差

DIF occurs when people with the same underlying level of a latent trait have different probabilities of endorsing an item based on their racial group. For example, on an IQ test, if two students (one white, one Black) have the same true cognitive ability, but the Black student has a lower probability of getting a particular question right due to stereotype threat, cultural content bias, or test format familiarity, that item exhibits DIF. Methods: IRT-based DIF analysis, logistic regression with interactions, or Mantel-Haenszel odds ratios. DIF doesn't mean an item is "bad"—some items may legitimately function differently if they probe constructs that differ across groups. But if your goal is to measure the same thing across groups, DIF items should be flagged or removed. This is particularly important for high-stakes testing (college admissions, professional licensure) where biased items directly affect life outcomes.

当具有相同基础潜在特征水平的人基于其种族群体对项目的认可概率不同时，会发生DIF。例如，在智商测试中，如果两个学生（一个白人，一个黑人）具有相同的真实认知能力，但由于刻板印象威胁、文化内容偏差或测试格式熟悉度，黑人学生的正确回答特定问题的概率较低，该项目表现出DIF。方法：基于IRT的DIF分析、与交互的逻辑回归或Mantel-Haenszel比值比。DIF不意味着一个项目"很糟糕"——如果某些项目合理探测在群体间不同的构造，可能会合理地不同地功能。但如果你的目标是在各群体间测量相同的东西，DIF项目应该被标记或移除。这对于高赌注测试（大学入学、专业执照）特别重要，其中有偏见的项目直接影响生活结果。

Analogy: A standardized test includes a question about sailing. High-income students (disproportionately white) have more experience sailing and higher probability of getting it right, even if overall test ability is the same. The item exhibits DIF by socioeconomic status. Similarly, items with cultural references (sports, music, books) may exhibit DIF by race.
类比：标准化测试包括一个关于航海的问题。高收入学生（不成比例的白人）有更多航海经验和更高的正确概率，即使整体测试能力相同。项目按社会经济地位表现出DIF。类似地，具有文化参考（运动、音乐、书籍）的项目可能因种族而表现出DIF。
When to use / not use: Test for DIF when: using published scales or standardized tests with racial comparisons; combining data from multiple sources; applying instruments developed on non-representative samples. Flag or remove DIF items if group comparisons are your focus. Don't interpret DIF as evidence that a group "actually scores lower"—it's evidence that your measurement has bias. Consider items both psychometrically and contextually (is it appropriate to remove an item if it's culturally valid for one group?).
何时用/不用：测试DIF当：使用发布的量表或标准化测试与种族比较；合并来自多个来源的数据；应用在非代表性样本上开发的工具。如果群体比较是你的重点，标记或移除DIF项目。不要将DIF解释为群体"实际得分更低"的证据——这是你的测量有偏见的证据。同时考虑项目的心理计量和语境（如果一个项目对一个群体在文化上有效，是否适合移除它？）。
Social science example: The SAT and ACT have been tested extensively for DIF. Some math items consistently have lower solve rates for female students with equivalent ability; some verbal items have lower rates for students from low-income backgrounds. Some items have DIF by race independent of socioeconomic status, suggesting test format or cultural content bias. This research contributed to ongoing debates about whether standardized tests measure academic ability or test-taking privilege.
社会科学例子：SAT和ACT已被广泛测试是否存在DIF。一些数学项目对具有等同能力的女性学生的解决率始终较低；一些口头项目对低收入背景的学生的解决率较低。一些项目在种族上独立于社会经济地位而表现出DIF，这表明测试格式或文化内容偏差。这项研究有助于关于标准化测试是否测量学术能力或应试特权的持续辩论。

Administrative Data Bias: Selection and Representation

行政数据偏差：选择与代表性

Police stops, arrests, convictions, hospital admissions, and credit approvals are recorded in administrative data. These seem like objective facts—but they measure enforcement, not underlying rates. A dataset of "people stopped by police" doesn't measure who committed crimes; it measures police behavior, department resources, and neighborhood deployment. Using police stop data to predict recidivism perpetuates the original bias: the people arrested are not a random sample of lawbreakers but a biased sample of people law enforcement chose to arrest. This is "algorithmic redlining"—training a predictive model on biased data produces biased predictions. Hospital admissions data reflects healthcare-seeking behavior and insurance status as much as disease prevalence. Credit applications reflect who applies for credit, not who would be a good loan candidate. The solution: use these data to study the institution (police, medical system, lenders), not as proxy for the underlying phenomenon.

警察拦截、逮捕、定罪、医院入院和信用批准都记录在行政数据中。这些看起来像客观事实——但它们测量执法，而不是基础率。"被警察拦截的人"的数据集不测量谁犯罪；它测量警察行为、部门资源和邻域部署。使用警察停止数据来预测再犯会使原始偏见永久化：被逮捕的人不是随机的违法者样本，而是执法机构选择逮捕的偏差样本。这是"算法红线"——在有偏见的数据上训练预测模型会产生有偏见的预测。医院入院数据反映寻求医疗行为和保险状况与疾病患病率一样多。信用申请反映谁申请信用，而不是谁会成为好的贷款候选人。解决方案：使用这些数据研究机构（警察、医疗系统、贷款方），而不是作为基础现象的代理。

Analogy: Studying which students get detention by looking only at detention records doesn't measure who misbehaves—it measures school discipline policy and which students are subject to closer surveillance. If Black students are detained at higher rates, you've measured racial disparities in discipline, not racial differences in behavior.
类比：仅通过查看拘留记录来研究哪些学生被拘留，不会测量谁表现不当——它测量学校纪律政策以及哪些学生接受更严格的监督。如果黑人学生的拘留率较高，你已经测量了纪律中的种族差异，而不是行为中的种族差异。
When to use / not use: Use administrative data to study the system (policing patterns, medical practice, lending criteria) not the underlying social phenomenon. When forced to use administrative data as a proxy for the underlying phenomenon, be explicit about selection bias and how it biases your estimates. Include alternative measures (surveys, audits, or experimental data) whenever possible. Don't train predictive algorithms on biased administrative data for consequential decisions without auditing for disparate impact.
何时用/不用：使用行政数据研究系统（警务模式、医学实践、借贷标准），而不是基础社会现象。当被迫使用行政数据作为基础现象的代理时，要明确选择偏见以及它如何使你的估计有偏。尽可能包括替代措施（调查、审计或实验数据）。不要在有偏见的行政数据上训练预测算法以做出有后果的决定，而不审计不同的影响。
Social science example: Propublica's investigation of COMPAS (a commercial risk assessment algorithm used in criminal courts) found that it mislabeled Black defendants as high-risk at much higher rates than white defendants with similar criminal histories. The algorithm was trained on historical conviction data—which reflects biased policing and prosecution, not actual crime risk. Fairness corrections are possible but trade off different definitions of fairness, revealing deeper questions about whether algorithms should match historical bias or try to correct it.
社会科学例子：ProPublica对COMPAS（在刑事法庭中使用的商业风险评估算法）的调查发现，它将黑人被告错误标记为高风险的速率远高于具有相似刑事历史的白人被告。该算法是在历史定罪数据上训练的——这反映了有偏见的警务和起诉，而不是实际犯罪风险。公平性更正是可能的，但权衡了不同的公平定义，揭示了关于算法是否应该匹配历史偏见或尝试更正的更深层问题。

Critical Quantitative Methodology and the Politics of Data

批判性量化方法与数据政治

The problem: Quantitative research is often presented as neutral and objective—numbers don't lie. But numbers are made: someone decided which categories count as "race," which variables to measure, which outcomes matter, which comparisons to highlight. These choices reflect power, politics, and interests. QuantCrit (quantitative critical race theory) asks: whose interests are served by these choices? What stories do we tell with data, and what stories do we silence?

问题：量化研究常被呈现为中立和客观——数字不会说谎。但数字是被制造的：有人决定了哪些类别算作"种族"，测量哪些变量，哪些结果很重要，要突出哪些比较。这些选择反映了权力、政治和利益。QuantCrit（量化批判种族理论）问：这些选择为谁的利益服务？我们用数据讲述了什么故事，我们沉默了什么故事？

QuantCrit: Numbers as Social Construction, Not Neutral Facts

QuantCrit：数字作为社会建构，而非中立事实

Quantitative critical race theory (QuantCrit), developed by scholars like Dumas, Kelley-Bowman, and Nelson, starts with a simple premise: numbers are not neutral. Data collection reflects choices about what to count, who to count, and what counts as valid measurement. Census categories are political choices (should we count people by race, ethnicity, ancestry, identity, or some combination?). Regression controls are political choices (should we adjust for income, which removes discrimination from the estimate, or leave it unadjusted, which includes it?). Statistical significance thresholds (why p<.05?) are conventional, not inevitable. Even "objectivity" is a value choice—research claiming objectivity often obscures its assumptions. QuantCrit scholarship reveals how quantitative research has been used to justify racial hierarchies: measured IQ "differences" that ignore measurement bias, racial genetic theories long-discredited but still influential, criminal risk algorithms trained on biased data. The response is not to abandon quantification but to do it more carefully, reflexively, and with awareness of power.

量化批判种族理论（QuantCrit），由Dumas、Kelley-Bowman和Nelson等学者发展，始于一个简单的前提：数字并非中立。数据收集反映了关于计数什么、计数谁以及什么算作有效测量的选择。人口普查类别是政治选择（我们应该按种族、族裔、祖先、身份还是某种组合来计数人？）。回归控制是政治选择（我们应该调整收入，这从估计中移除歧视，还是不调整，这包括它？）。统计显著性阈值（为什么p<.05？）是约定俗成的，而不是不可避免的。甚至"客观性"也是价值选择——声称客观性的研究常常掩盖其假设。QuantCrit奖学金揭示了量化研究如何被用来证明种族等级制度是合理的：测量的智商"差异"忽视了测量偏差，种族遗传理论长期被否认但仍有影响力，刑事风险算法在有偏见的数据上训练。回应不是放弃量化，而是更谨慎、自我反思和意识权力地进行。

Analogy: Sports statistics appear objective—a batting average is a batting average. But what counts as an "at-bat" is a rule—a batter hit by a pitch is not counted as an at-bat, favoring batters who get hit. Different sports have different rules about what events count. Similarly, quantitative research has rules about what counts as an outcome, a confound, or a mechanism. These rules look neutral but carry consequences.
类比：体育统计看起来是客观的——击球率就是击球率。但什么算作"一个打击"是一个规则——被球击中的击球手不计为打击，有利于被击中的击球手。不同的运动对什么事件计数有不同的规则。类似地，量化研究有关于什么算作结果、混淆或机制的规则。这些规则看起来是中立的，但包含后果。
When to use / not use: Embrace QuantCrit not as a reason to abandon quantitative research, but as a reason to be more careful. Document your choices: why these variables, why this sample, why this outcome? Consider alternative specifications and how they change results. Acknowledge limitations and assumptions. Use quantitative work alongside qualitative work, community knowledge, and historical context. Don't present quantitative results as if they're the final word on truth—they're evidence from a particular angle, with particular assumptions.
何时用/不用：拥抱QuantCrit不是放弃量化研究的理由，而是更谨慎的理由。记录你的选择：为什么这些变量、为什么这个样本、为什么这个结果？考虑替代规格以及它们如何改变结果。承认局限性和假设。将量化工作与定性工作、社区知识和历史背景一起使用。不要将量化结果呈现为好像它们是真理的最后一句话——它们是来自特定角度的证据，具有特定的假设。
Social science example: The "bell curve debate" in psychology/sociology—claims about racial differences in IQ. These studies measure what? Test-taking ability, cultural knowledge, educational access, stereotype threat? The statistical result (some racial groups score higher on IQ tests) is reproducible but carries multiple interpretations. QuantCrit analysis asks: why are we asking this question, who benefits from different answers, and what power structures are reinforced by claiming genetic racial differences in intelligence? The same regression coefficient can be read as evidence of discrimination or evidence of group differences depending on your theoretical frame—the "objectivity" of the number hides the non-objectivity of interpretation.
社会科学例子：心理学/社会学中的"钟形曲线辩论"——关于智商中种族差异的声明。这些研究测量什么？应试能力、文化知识、教育获取、刻板印象威胁？统计结果（某些种族群体在智商测试中得分更高）是可复制的，但有多种解释。QuantCrit分析问：为什么我们要提这个问题，不同的答案对谁有利，声称智力中的遗传种族差异强化了什么权力结构？同一回归系数可以根据你的理论框架读作歧视证据或群体差异证据——数字的"客观性"隐藏了解释的非客观性。

Participatory and Community-Centered Quantitative Research

参与性和以社区为中心的量化研究

A QuantCrit approach to practice means involving affected communities in research design, not just data collection. Communities have knowledge about their own inequalities and are often harmed by research that extracts data, publishes findings, and leaves. Participatory action research (PAR) makes communities partners in analysis and interpretation. What questions do community members want answered? What variables matter to them? How should results be used—to justify policy changes, to challenge representations, to support advocacy? This changes what gets counted: traditional research on "achievement gaps" in schools counts test scores; community-centered research might count school funding, teacher quality, and student agency. Partnering with communities takes time, requires shared power over interpretation, and means accepting that not all findings will be publishable in conventional outlets. But it produces research more likely to matter to the people affected and less likely to reinforce the power structures that created inequality.

QuantCrit方法实践意味着让受影响的社区参与研究设计，而不仅仅是数据收集。社区对自己的不平等有了解，并且经常因提取数据、发布发现和离开的研究而受害。参与行动研究（PAR）使社区成为分析和解释的伙伴。社区成员想回答什么问题？什么变量对他们很重要？结果应该如何使用——为政策变化辩护、挑战表述、支持倡导？这改变了计数的内容：学校中"成就差距"的传统研究计算测试分数；以社区为中心的研究可能计算学校资金、教师质量和学生能动性。与社区合作需要时间，需要在解释上共享权力，意味着接受不是所有发现都会在传统出版物中发表。但它产生了更可能对受影响的人至关重要的研究，并且不太可能强化导致不平等的权力结构。

Analogy: Traditional extractive research is like a journalist parachuting into a town, interviewing residents without context, and publishing a story that misrepresents their lives. Participatory research is more like living in a community, learning its history, and working with residents to tell stories that reflect their knowledge and serve their interests.
类比：传统提取研究就像记者空投到一个城镇，在没有背景的情况下采访居民，发布误传了他们生活的故事。参与式研究更像是在社区中生活，学习其历史，与居民合作讲述反映他们知识并为他们利益服务的故事。
When to use / not use: Involve communities when your research affects them (policy-relevant research on inequality, public health, education, criminal justice). Don't involve communities if your research is purely theoretical or has no external validity implications. Set expectations clearly: participatory research is slower, shared power can conflict with publication pressures, and some findings may not be publishable in conventional venues. Build in time for community feedback and interpretation, not just data collection.
何时用/不用：当你的研究影响社区时涉及社区（与不平等相关的政策相关研究、公共卫生、教育、刑事司法）。如果你的研究是纯粹理论的或没有外部有效性含义，不要涉及社区。清楚地设定期望：参与式研究较慢，共享权力可能与出版压力冲突，一些发现可能不会在常规场所发表。为社区反馈和解释投入时间，而不仅仅是数据收集。
Social science example: Participatory research on housing instability in a low-income neighborhood. Instead of researchers defining "housing instability" as missed rent payments, community members identified that stable housing requires not just affordable rent but also safe conditions, proximity to work/school, and freedom from harassment. Quantifying these variables—safety, commute time, harassment frequency—revealed a different picture than traditional measures of "homelessness" vs. "housed." Results informed local advocacy for tenant protections, which is how research should matter.
社会科学例子：关于低收入邻域住房不稳定的参与式研究。研究人员没有将"住房不稳定"定义为错过的租金支付，社区成员确定了稳定的住房不仅需要负担得起的租金，还需要安全条件、靠近工作/学校和免受骚扰。量化这些变量——安全、通勤时间、骚扰频率——与传统的"无家可归"与"有住房"的测量相比，揭示了不同的图景。结果为租户保护的当地倡导提供了信息，这是研究应该如何重要。

Reflexivity, Positionality, and Limits of What We Can Know

自我反思、位置性与我们能知道的东西的限制

Reflexivity means examining how your own position (race, class, gender, disciplinary training) shapes what you can see and know. A researcher from a wealthy background studying poverty sees poverty through a particular lens. A Black scholar studying racial discrimination brings different knowledge and questions than a white scholar. Neither position gives access to "truth"—both are partial, and mixing perspectives is stronger than assuming any single perspective is objective. Positionality is not bias to eliminate but a feature of knowledge production to acknowledge and work with. In practice, this means: documenting assumptions, considering alternative interpretations, checking findings with people in the communities you study, and being honest about limitations. It also means acknowledging power imbalances in research: the researcher has power to frame questions, interpret answers, and publish findings. Being reflexive about this doesn't make the imbalance disappear but makes it visible and enables more ethical choices.

自我反思意味着审视你自己的位置（种族、阶级、性别、学科培训）如何塑造你能看到和知道的东西。来自富裕背景的贫困研究者通过特定的镜头看待贫困。黑人学者研究种族歧视比白人学者带来不同的知识和问题。两个位置都不能获得"真理"——都是部分的，混合观点比假设任何单个观点是客观的更强。位置性不是要消除的偏见，而是知识生产的特点，要承认和使用。实际上，这意味着：记录假设、考虑替代解释、与你研究的社区中的人检查发现，以及诚实对待限制。它也意味着承认研究中的权力失衡：研究人员有权框架化问题、解释答案和发布发现。对此进行自我反思不会使失衡消失，但会使其可见并启用更道德的选择。

Analogy: Three people describe a landscape: one standing on the mountain top, one in the valley, one on the horizon. All three observations are true and partial. The view from each position is different and valuable. The top observer sees the whole landscape but misses the granular detail of the valley. Pretending there's a view-from-nowhere is false; acknowledging perspectives and combining them is better.
类比：三个人描述一个风景：一个站在山顶上，一个在山谷中，一个在地平线上。所有三个观察都是真实的和部分的。从每个位置的观点都不同且有价值。顶部观察者看到了整个景观，但错过了山谷的细粒度细节。假装存在无处-的观点是错误的；承认观点并结合它们更好。
When to use / not use: Reflexive practice is useful for all research, essential for research on sensitive topics (race, inequality, trauma). Write a positionality statement: who are you, what's your relationship to this topic, how might your position shape your research? Identify and acknowledge blind spots. Don't use reflexivity as an excuse for relativism (all perspectives are equally valid)—some interpretations are better supported by evidence than others. Reflexivity is about intellectual humility, not about denying that evidence matters.
何时用/不用：自我反思实践对所有研究都有用，对敏感话题研究（种族、不平等、创伤）必不可少。写一个位置性陈述：你是谁、你与这个话题的关系是什么、你的位置可能如何塑造你的研究？识别和承认盲点。不要使用自我反思作为相对主义的借口（所有观点都同样有效）——一些解释比其他解释由证据更好地支持。自我反思是关于智力谦虚，而不是关于否认证据的重要性。
Social science example: Studies of police violence often ask "what factors predict officer use of force?" which frames the question as what victim characteristics provoke force. A reflexive reframing: "what factors predict police deployment of force and how do officer characteristics, training, and accountability structures shape this?" The second question, asked by researchers aware of power imbalances, centers the institution rather than the victim. Both questions can be addressed quantitatively, but the second is more likely to generate findings useful for reform.
社会科学例子：关于警察暴力的研究常常提问"什么因素预测警察使用武力？"这框架化了将问题作为受害者什么特征激发力量。一个自我反思的重新框架化："什么因素预测警察部署武力，警察特征、培训和问责结构如何塑造这一点？"第二个问题，由意识到权力失衡的研究人员提出，以机构而不是受害者为中心。两个问题都可以定量解决，但第二个更可能产生对改革有用的发现。
