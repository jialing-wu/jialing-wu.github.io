The Rational Choice Framework: Preferences, Constraints, and Optimization

理性选择框架：偏好、约束与最优化

The problem: You observe political candidates moving toward the center, or countries signing treaties, or people joining environmental groups. How can you predict whether these actions will happen? The rational choice framework offers a way to model deliberate decision-making under constraints. It assumes people have preferences that are consistent and transitive, and that they choose actions to maximize expected utility given their beliefs about what others will do.

问题：你观察到政治候选人向中心移动，或国家签署条约，或人们加入环保组织。你怎样才能预测这些行为是否会发生？理性选择框架提供了一种对约束下故意决策进行建模的方法。它假设人们的偏好是一致和可传递的，他们选择行动来最大化预期效用，考虑到他们对他人会做什么的信念。

Core Assumptions: Completeness, Transitivity, and Utility Maximization

核心假设：完整性、及物性与效用最大化

The rational choice framework rests on three assumptions. First, preferences are complete: for any two options A and B, a person either prefers A, prefers B, or is indifferent. No "incomparable" preferences where you can't rank two options—this is actually violated often (most people can't rank poetry vs. pizza). Second, preferences are transitive: if you prefer A to B and B to C, you prefer A to C. Intransitive preferences allow preference cycles where you prefer A to B, B to C, and C to A—money-pumping paradoxes that rational choice rules out. Third, actors maximize expected utility: they choose the action that gives the highest expected payoff, computing the utility of each outcome weighted by its probability. What "rational" means: it's about consistency, not selfishness or intelligence. A person maximizing the welfare of their community is rational if they do so consistently; so is a person maximizing personal wealth. Rational choice doesn't describe how people actually think—it's a normative model of how consistent preferences should lead to choices, useful as a baseline for understanding when and why people deviate.

理性选择框架基于三个假设。首先，偏好是完整的：对于任何两个选项A和B，一个人要么喜欢A，要么喜欢B，要么无差别。没有"无法比较"的偏好，你无法排列两个选项——这实际上经常被违反（大多数人无法排列诗歌与披萨）。其次，偏好是可传递的：如果你喜欢A胜过B，B胜过C，你喜欢A胜过C。不可传递的偏好允许偏好循环，你喜欢A胜过B，B胜过C，C胜过A——理性选择排除的金钱泵悖论。第三，行动者最大化预期效用：他们选择给予最高预期回报的行动，计算每个结果的效用乘以其概率。"理性"的含义：这是关于一致性，而不是自私或智力。在社区福利最大化中一致地做的人是理性的；个人财富最大化的人也是。理性选择不描述人们实际的想法——它是一致偏好应该如何导致选择的规范模型，作为理解人们何时以及为什么偏离的基线很有用。

Analogy: A rational choice model of restaurant ordering. You have preferences over menu items (pizza, salad, tacos), you know the prices and ingredients, you choose the item that maximizes your utility given your budget. But this ignores: your mood varies, what your friend orders influences what you want, the waiter's recommendation shifts your preferences, you don't know how hungry you are until after eating. Real choice involves context, emotion, and social influence that rational choice axioms exclude.
类比：一个关于餐厅点餐的理性选择模型。你对菜单项目（披萨、沙拉、玉米卷）有偏好，你知道价格和成分，你选择在你的预算范围内最大化你效用的项目。但这忽视了：你的心情变化，你的朋友点什么会影响你想要什么，服务员的推荐改变你的偏好，你在吃之前不知道你有多饿。真实的选择涉及理性选择公理排除的背景、情感和社会影响。
When to use / not use: Use rational choice to generate precise predictions about how institutional design or incentives affect behavior. Use it to understand strategic situations with stable preferences and clear payoffs (market behavior, voting under specific electoral systems, arms races). Don't use it for emotions, identity-based choices, or where preferences are unstable or socially constructed. Don't assume actual people follow rational choice axioms—they're a model, not a description of reality.
何时用/不用：使用理性选择来生成关于制度设计或激励如何影响行为的精确预测。使用它来理解具有稳定偏好和清晰回报的战略形势（市场行为、具体选举制度下的投票、军备竞赛）。不要将其用于情感、基于身份的选择或偏好不稳定或社会建构的地方。不要假设真实的人遵循理性选择公理——它们是一个模型，而不是现实的描述。
Social science example: Anthony Downs' (1957) "An Economic Theory of Democracy" models voters as rational choice actors. Voters have ideal policy positions, parties know this, parties choose positions to maximize votes. Key prediction: parties converge toward the median voter. In the left-right dimension, a two-party system predicts both parties move to the center because that's where the median voter is. This prediction holds surprisingly well for some elections (explaining why moderate candidates dominate two-party systems) but fails when voters have intense preferences about non-economic issues (abortion, immigration) or when primaries select candidates before general elections.
社会科学例子：Anthony Downs（1957）的《民主经济理论》将选民建模为理性选择行动者。选民有理想的政策立场，政党了解这一点，政党选择位置以最大化选票。关键预测：政党向中值选民收敛。在左右维度中，两党制预测两个政党都向中心移动，因为那是中值选民所在的地方。这种预测对某些选举的预测意外地很好（解释为什么温和候选人在两党制中主导），但当选民对非经济问题（堕胎、移民）有强烈偏好或初选在大选前选择候选人时失败。

Revealed Preference and Indifference Curves

显示偏好与无差别曲线

We can't directly observe preferences, but we can infer them from choices—the "revealed preference" approach. If you choose A when both A and B are available and equally priced, you've revealed that you prefer A. Indifference curves map combinations of two goods that yield equal utility: if I'm indifferent between 2 cups of coffee and 3 cups of tea, those bundles lie on the same indifference curve. The slope of the indifference curve is the marginal rate of substitution—how much tea you're willing to give up for one more coffee. The budget line is where income and prices constrain your choices. Rational choice predicts you choose the bundle where the indifference curve is tangent to the budget line—where you're maximizing utility given constraints. This geometry is elegant but limited: it works beautifully for goods with continuous substitutability, less well for discrete choices (voting, joining groups) or choices under uncertainty. For discrete choices, you need a different framework—multinomial logit or probit models—which translate the same logic into probabilistic choice.

我们无法直接观察偏好，但我们可以从选择中推断它们——"显示偏好"方法。如果你在A和B都可用且价格相等时选择A，你就显示你更喜欢A。无差别曲线映射两种商品的组合，产生相等的效用：如果我对2杯咖啡和3杯茶无差别，这些束就在相同的无差别曲线上。无差别曲线的斜率是边际替代率——你愿意放弃多少茶来获得一杯咖啡。预算线是收入和价格约束你选择的地方。理性选择预测你选择无差别曲线与预算线相切的束——你在给定约束下最大化效用。这个几何是优雅的但有限的：对于具有连续可替代性的商品，它工作得很好；对于离散选择（投票、加入组织）或不确定选择的选择，工作得更少。对于离散选择，你需要一个不同的框架——多项逻辑或概率模型——这将相同的逻辑翻译成概率选择。

Analogy: Your food choices reveal preferences over nutrition and taste. If you buy whole-grain bread when white bread is cheaper, you reveal you prefer health to cost savings. The indifference curve captures the tradeoff: how much extra cost are you willing to pay for whole grains? This works for continuous goods but breaks down for choices like "should I go to college?"—there's no indifference curve for college vs. no college because you can't have 0.5 college.
类比：你的食物选择揭示了对营养和味道的偏好。如果你在白面包更便宜时购买全麦面包，你就表明你更喜欢健康而不是成本储蓄。无差别曲线捕捉了权衡：你愿意为全麦额外支付多少？这对连续商品有效，但对"我应该上大学吗？"之类的选择崩溃——因为你不能有0.5大学，所以没有关于大学与无大学的无差别曲线。
When to use / not use: Use indifference curves to visualize tradeoffs in continuous choice problems (consumption bundles, time allocation). They're particularly useful pedagogically—they make the constraint-maximization problem visual. Don't use them for understanding voting, group participation, or other discrete choices where the framework doesn't fit. Remember that indifference curves are inferred from behavior, not directly observed—if behavior doesn't match indifference curve predictions, it might be because preferences are unstable, social factors matter, or people use decision heuristics rather than utility maximization.
何时用/不用：使用无差别曲线来可视化连续选择问题中的权衡（消费束、时间分配）。它们在教学上特别有用——它们使约束最大化问题可见。不要使用它们来理解投票、群体参与或框架不适用的其他离散选择。请记住，无差别曲线是从行为推断的，不是直接观察的——如果行为与无差别曲线预测不匹配，可能是因为偏好不稳定、社会因素很重要，或者人们使用决策启发式而不是效用最大化。
Social science example: Labor supply models use indifference curves over income and leisure. Workers choose how much to work given wages and other income. The indifference curve maps the tradeoff: how much additional income needed to give up one hour of leisure? When wages rise, workers could work more (income effect dominates) or less (substitution effect dominates—leisure becomes more valuable as your hourly wage rises). Empirically, labor supply often responds differently to temporary vs. permanent income changes, violating the model's assumption that preferences over consumption/leisure are stable.
社会科学例子：劳动力供应模型使用关于收入和闲暇的无差别曲线。工人根据工资和其他收入选择工作多少。无差别曲线映射权衡：放弃一小时闲暇需要多少额外收入？当工资上升时，工人可以工作更多（收入效应占主导）或更少（替代效应占主导——当你的小时工资上升时，闲暇变得更有价值）。在经验上，劳动力供应通常对临时与永久收入变化的响应不同，违反了模型关于消费/闲暇偏好稳定的假设。

Strategic Interaction and Game Theory

策略性互动与博弈论

The problem: Individual rationality assumes you know what others will do (or can estimate probabilities). But in strategic situations, what others do depends on what they think you'll do, which depends on what they think you think they'll do. Your best choice depends on their choices; their best choices depend on yours. You need game theory to think through this circular interdependence and find equilibrium predictions—where no one can improve by unilaterally changing their behavior.

问题：个体理性假设你知道他人会做什么（或可以估计概率）。但在战略形势中，他人所做的取决于他们认为你会做什么，这取决于他们认为你认为他们会做什么。你的最佳选择取决于他们的选择；他们的最佳选择取决于你的。你需要博弈论来思考这种循环相互依赖，并找到均衡预测——没有人可以通过单方面改变他们的行为来改进。

Normal Form Games and Nash Equilibrium

法线博弈与纳什均衡

A game has players, strategies (actions available to each player), and payoffs (what each player gets from each combination of strategies). Normal form displays all combinations in a matrix. A simple example: the Prisoner's Dilemma, where two suspects face a choice to cooperate (stay silent) or defect (confess). Each gets a payoff depending on what the other does. The payoff structure creates a dilemma: mutual cooperation is best for both, but each player's dominant strategy is to defect (confess gets a better payoff regardless of what the other does). The result: both defect, worse for both than mutual cooperation. A Nash equilibrium is a combination of strategies where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing strategy. In the Prisoner's Dilemma, mutual defection is the Nash equilibrium—even though mutual cooperation would be better for both, neither player has incentive to deviate. Not all games have a pure strategy Nash equilibrium (where everyone plays a specific strategy). In rock-paper-scissors, there's no pure equilibrium because scissors beats rock, rock beats paper, paper beats scissors—a cycle. The equilibrium is mixed: play each strategy with equal probability (1/3). Mixed strategy equilibrium means players randomize, making themselves unpredictable to opponents.

一个博弈有参与者、策略（每个参与者可用的行为）和回报（每个参与者从每个策略组合中获得什么）。法线形式在矩阵中显示所有组合。一个简单的例子：囚徒困境，两个嫌疑人面临合作（保持沉默）或背叛（认罪）的选择。每个人的回报取决于另一个人所做的。回报结构产生了一个困境：相互合作对两者都是最好的，但每个参与者的主导策略是背叛（认罪获得更好的回报，无论另一个人做什么）。结果：两人都背叛，两人都比相互合作更糟。纳什均衡是策略的组合，其中没有参与者可以通过单方面改变策略来改进他们的回报。在囚徒困境中，相互背叛是纳什均衡——即使相互合作对两者都更好，但两个参与者都没有偏离的动机。并非所有游戏都有纯策略纳什均衡（每个人都玩特定策略的地方）。在剪刀石头布中，没有纯均衡，因为剪刀击败石头，石头击败纸张，纸张击败剪刀——一个循环。均衡是混合的：以相等的概率（1/3）播放每个策略。混合策略均衡意味着参与者随机化，使自己对对手无法预测。

Analogy: Rock-paper-scissors has no pure strategy equilibrium because you can't have a dominant strategy in a cycle. In a pure equilibrium, you'd always play rock (because you know your opponent plays scissors), but then they'd switch to paper. So you both end up randomizing. This explains why poker pros randomize when they're indifferent between two plays—to make themselves unpredictable.
类比：剪刀石头布在循环中没有纯策略均衡，因为你不能有主导策略。在纯均衡中，你总是会玩石头（因为你知道你的对手玩剪刀），但随后他们会切换到纸张。所以你们都最终会随机化。这解释了为什么扑克职业选手在他们对两种玩法无差别时随机化——为了使自己无法预测。
When to use / not use: Use normal form for simultaneous-move games (voting, bidding, choosing products). Use extensive form for sequential games (bargaining, entry-deterrence). Find Nash equilibrium to make predictions about play, but acknowledge it's a prediction, not a guarantee—people don't always play Nash equilibrium, especially in one-shot games or when payoffs are small relative to strategic uncertainty. Test game-theoretic predictions experimentally or in observational data when possible.
何时用/不用：对同步移动游戏使用法线形式（投票、竞标、选择产品）。对序列游戏使用扩展形式（谈判、进入阻止）。找到纳什均衡以对游戏进行预测，但承认这是预测，而不是保证——人们不总是玩纳什均衡，尤其是在一次性游戏或回报相对于战略不确定性很小时。尽可能在实验或观察数据中测试博弈论预测。
Social science example: Electoral competition in primary elections is an entry game. Many candidates face a choice: enter (pay campaign costs and risk losing) or not enter. If you expect the front-runner to win, you stay out. But if many candidates drop out, the front-runner looks inevitable, encouraging more to drop out. Sequential entry decisions create a bandwagon. The Nash equilibrium depends on who moves when and what information is available. Changes to primary rules (order of voting, frontloading certain states, instant runoff voting) change the equilibrium predictions.
社会科学例子：初选中的选举竞争是进入游戏。许多候选人面临选择：进入（支付竞选费用和风险失败）或不进入。如果你期望领先者赢，你就退出。但是，如果许多候选人退出，领先者看起来是不可避免的，鼓励更多退出。序列进入决定创造了一个乐队。纳什均衡取决于谁何时移动以及什么信息可用。对初选规则的改变（投票顺序、某些州的最前沿、即时决胜投票）改变均衡预测。

Common Games and Their Lessons

常见游戏及其教训

Several games model common strategic situations. Prisoner's Dilemma: both players prefer mutual cooperation but mutual defection is the Nash equilibrium. Models arms races, environmental degradation, oligopolistic competition. Stag Hunt: hunting a stag requires cooperation (high payoff), but each can catch a hare alone (medium payoff). The difference from Prisoner's Dilemma: mutual cooperation is an equilibrium, but so is mutual defection. Risk dominates payoff dominance—rational actors choose the risky stag hunt if they trust others, or the safe hare if uncertainty dominates. Models trust problems in society. Chicken (hawk-dove): two drivers speed toward each other; whoever swerves first loses face. Both want to avoid collision (worst outcome) but also want the other to swerve. Asymmetric game with commitment advantages—if you can convince others you won't swerve, they must. Models international conflict, timing of business decisions. Battle of the Sexes: a couple wants to spend evening together but prefers different activities (concert vs. football). Coordination game (being together beats being apart) but with conflicting preferences. Multiple equilibria—tradition or power determines which happens. All these games have different structures with different implications for what's rational and what happens in equilibrium.

几个游戏模拟常见的战略形势。囚徒困境：两个参与者都喜欢相互合作，但相互背叛是纳什均衡。建模军备竞赛、环境恶化、寡头竞争。雌鹿狩猎：猎取雌鹿需要合作（高回报），但每一个都可以单独抓到野兔（中等回报）。与囚徒困境的区别：相互合作是均衡，相互背叛也是。风险支配回报支配——理性行动者如果信任他人会选择有风险的雌鹿狩猎，或者如果不确定性占主导地位会选择安全的野兔。建模社会中的信任问题。鸡（鹰鸽）：两个驾驶员相向加速；谁先转向就丧失颜面。都想避免碰撞（最坏结果）但也希望对方转向。具有承诺优势的不对称游戏——如果你能让他人相信你不会转向，他们必须转向。建模国际冲突、业务决定时机。两性之战：一对夫妇想在一起度过晚上，但更喜欢不同的活动（音乐会与足球）。协调游戏（在一起击败分开）但偏好冲突。多个均衡——传统或权力决定什么发生。所有这些游戏都有不同的结构，对什么是理性的以及均衡中发生的事情有不同的含义。

Analogy: Climate change is a Prisoner's Dilemma: countries cooperate on emissions (all better off) vs. defect (maximize national growth, but everyone worse off). Trade agreements are coordination games where countries both want mutually beneficial trade but prefer terms favoring themselves. International security is often Chicken: deterrence works if adversaries believe you won't back down, but misperception creates risk.
类比：气候变化是囚徒困境：国家合作排放（都更好）对背叛（最大化国家增长，但每个人都更差）。贸易协议是协调游戏，国家都想要互利贸易，但偏好对自己有利的条款。国际安全通常是鸡：如果对手相信你不会退缩，威慑有效，但误解会产生风险。
When to use / not use: Choose the game structure that matches your problem. If both cooperation and defection are equilibria, trust matters (Stag Hunt). If only defection is equilibrium, you need institutions or reputation to sustain cooperation. If multiple equilibria exist with different payoffs, explain how one equilibrium is selected (focal points, history, institutions). Avoid over-simplifying real situations into games—actual strategic problems involve incomplete information, reputational concerns, and non-standard payoffs.
何时用/不用：选择与你的问题相匹配的游戏结构。如果合作和背叛都是均衡，信任很重要（雌鹿狩猎）。如果只有背叛是均衡，你需要机构或声誉来维持合作。如果存在多个具有不同回报的均衡，解释如何选择一个均衡（焦点、历史、机构）。避免将真实情况过度简化为游戏——实际战略问题涉及不完全信息、声誉问题和非标准回报。
Social science example: Baron & Ferejohn (1989) model legislative bargaining as an extensive-form game. A proposer offers a bill; if it passes (minimum winning coalition supports), everyone gets their share; if it fails, someone else proposes next round with same rules. Backward induction predicts the proposer gets disproportionate share (everyone in the coalition gets minimum, proposer takes rest), and this shapes voting power asymmetrically. Empirically, divided legislatures (where proposing power changes hands) show different outcomes than unified control—consistent with game theory predictions about how proposing power translates to legislative outcomes.
社会科学例子：Baron & Ferejohn（1989）将立法讨价还价建模为扩展形式的游戏。提议者提出一项法案；如果通过（最少获胜联盟支持），每个人都获得他们的份额；如果失败，有人下一轮以相同的规则提议。向后归纳预测提议者获得不成比例的份额（联盟中的每个人都获得最少，提议者获得其余），这非对称地塑造投票权。在实证上，分裂立法机关（提议权改变手的地方）显示与统一控制不同的结果——与博弈论关于提议权如何转化为立法结果的预测一致。

Limitations of Standard Game Theory

标准博弈论的局限

Game theory assumes common knowledge of the game (everyone knows the payoffs and strategies), perfect rationality, and stable preferences. Real strategic situations violate these. Information is often incomplete: you don't know opponents' payoffs or intentions (are they negotiating in good faith or stalling for time?). Rationality is bounded: computing best responses in complex games is cognitively difficult. Preferences shift based on context: framing matters (is a choice presented as a loss or gain?). Emotions matter: anger, fear, pride influence strategic choices independently of payoffs. Social preferences matter: most people have some altruism (willingness to help others) and fairness concerns (anger at inequality), which traditional game theory treats as non-existent. These extensions have been incorporated into behavioral game theory, but they complicate model predictions. A key limitation: in one-shot games or games with little repetition, equilibrium predictions often fail because people optimize inconsistently, focus on fairness, or have misperceptions. Game theory works better for repeated games, institutions where incentives are clear, and strategic situations where learning is possible.

博弈论假设对游戏的共同知识（每个人都知道回报和策略）、完全理性和稳定偏好。真实的战略形势违反了这些。信息通常不完整：你不知道对手的回报或意图（他们真诚谈判还是拖延时间？）。理性是有限的：计算复杂游戏中的最佳回应在认知上是困难的。偏好根据背景转变：框架很重要（选择是作为损失还是收益呈现的？）。情感很重要：愤怒、恐惧、骄傲独立于回报影响战略选择。社会偏好很重要：大多数人有某种利他主义（帮助他人的意愿）和公平性关注（对不平等的愤怒），传统博弈论将其视为不存在。这些扩展已纳入行为博弈论，但它们使模型预测复杂化。一个关键局限：在一次性游戏或重复很少的游戏中，均衡预测经常失败，因为人们优化不一致、关注公平或有误解。博弈论对重复游戏、激励明确的机构以及可能学习的战略形势更有效。

Analogy: Chess has perfect information and a game tree that's theoretically solvable (white moves, black responds, etc.), but practically unsolvable—too many branches. Poker adds incomplete information (hidden cards) and adds irrationality: bluffing works partly because people irrationally fold hands that might win. Real strategic situations are even messier—hidden information, emotions, misperceptions, bounded cognition.
类比：国际象棋具有完全信息和理论上可解决的游戏树（白色移动、黑色响应等），但实际上无法解决——太多分支。扑克增加了不完全信息（隐藏卡）并增加了非理性：虚张声势的工作部分是因为人们非理性地折叠可能赢的手。真实的战略形势更混乱——隐藏信息、情感、误解、有界认知。
When to use / not use: Use game theory for equilibrium analysis and institutional design (how to set up incentives). Don't rely on equilibrium predictions for one-shot games or games with low repetition—people often deviate. Supplement game theory with behavioral analysis (bounded rationality, fairness preferences, emotional responses). Use experimental data or qualitative evidence to understand actual strategic behavior when equilibrium predictions fail.
何时用/不用：使用博弈论进行均衡分析和制度设计（如何设置激励）。不要依赖一次性游戏或重复很少的游戏的均衡预测——人们经常偏离。用行为分析补充博弈论（有界理性、公平偏好、情感反应）。当均衡预测失败时，使用实验数据或定性证据来理解实际的战略行为。
Social science example: Ultimatum games show that game theory equilibrium predictions fail in one-shot games. Standard theory predicts proposer offers minimum acceptable payoff to responder (nearly zero) and keeps the rest. Empirically, proposers offer 40-50% of the pot, and responders reject unfair offers (near-zero proposals) even at cost to themselves. This "irrational" behavior is consistent with fairness preferences—people prefer equal splits and punish unfairness. Repeated games and reputation concerns explain more of actual behavior than one-shot equilibrium analysis.
社会科学例子：最后通牒游戏显示博弈论均衡预测在一次性游戏中失败。标准理论预测提议者为应答者提供最小可接受的回报（几乎为零），并保留其余。在经验上，提议者提供罐头的40-50%，应答者拒绝不公平的提议（接近零的提议），即使对自己有成本。这种"非理性"行为与公平偏好一致——人们更喜欢平等分割并惩罚不公平。重复游戏和声誉关注比一次性均衡分析解释了更多实际行为。

Collective Action Problems and the Tragedy of the Commons

集体行动问题与公地悲剧

The problem: Everyone wants clean air, but each person has incentive to pollute (avoiding pollution costs money). Everyone wants forests to survive, but each logger has incentive to cut more trees today (before someone else does). Everyone benefits from a strong union, but each worker has incentive to free-ride (get union benefits without paying dues). Individually rational choices produce collectively irrational outcomes: the commons is destroyed, the union collapses. How do groups overcome this?

问题：每个人都想要清洁空气，但每个人都有污染的动机（避免污染需要花钱）。每个人都希望森林存活，但每个伐木工人都有动机今天砍伐更多树木（在其他人这样做之前）。每个人都从强大的工会中受益，但每个工人都有搭便车的动机（获得工会福利而不支付会费）。个体理性的选择产生集体不理性的结果：公地被摧毁，工会崩溃。群体如何克服这一点？

The Logic of Collective Action: Olson's Paradox

集体行动的逻辑：Olson的悖论

Mancur Olson (1965) posed a puzzle: large groups with common interests often fail to organize, while small groups do. Why? Because of free-riding. A public good (clean air, a strong union, a better government) is non-excludable (you can't prevent non-contributors from benefiting) and non-rivalrous (one person's benefit doesn't reduce others'). If a union successfully raises wages, all workers benefit whether or not they paid dues—creating incentive to free-ride. In a large group, your contribution is negligible (the outcome is nearly the same whether you contribute), so rational actors don't bother. Small groups have higher stakes—your contribution is noticeable, free-riding is visible. Olson's solution: selective incentives. Unions coerce membership; environmental groups offer t-shirts; professional associations provide credentials. By making group membership privately beneficial, selective incentives overcome free-riding. But selective incentives are not always available: how do you provide selective incentives for democracy, international cooperation, or norm compliance? The logic extends to common pool resources (fish stocks, forests, water) where individuals benefit from extraction but the group suffers from depletion. Each fisher wants to catch as many fish as possible; collectively, they deplete the stock. This is the tragedy of the commons: individual rationality produces collective tragedy.

Mancur Olson（1965）提出了一个谜题：拥有共同利益的大群体往往不能组织，而小群体会组织。为什么？因为搭便车。公共商品（清洁空气、强大的工会、更好的政府）是不可排斥的（你不能阻止非贡献者受益）和不具有竞争性的（一个人的利益不会减少他人的）。如果工会成功地提高工资，所有工人都会受益，无论他们是否支付会费——创造搭便车的动机。在大群体中，你的贡献是微不足道的（无论你是否贡献，结果几乎相同），所以理性行动者不会费劲。小群体的利害关系较高——你的贡献是显著的，搭便车是可见的。Olson的解决方案：选择性激励。工会强制会员；环保组织提供T恤；专业协会提供证书。通过使群体成员身份私人有益，选择性激励克服了搭便车。但并非总是有选择性激励：你如何为民主、国际合作或规范遵从提供选择性激励？逻辑扩展到共同池资源（鱼类种群、森林、水），个人从开采中受益，但群体因枯竭而受害。每条鱼想抓尽可能多的鱼；共同地，他们耗尽库存。这是公地悲剧：个体理性产生集体悲剧。

Analogy: Overfishing in real time: each fisher rationally maximizes their catch, the stock depletes, and suddenly no one can fish. It's rational to fish as much as possible before the stock runs out, but collectively this produces rapid depletion. Compare to a regulated fishery with catch quotas—individual rationality is constrained by institutions, preventing tragedy.
类比：实时过度捕捞：每条鱼合理地最大化了他们的捕获，库存耗尽，突然没有人可以钓鱼。在库存用尽之前尽可能多地捕鱼是合理的，但共同地这产生了快速枯竭。与有捕获配额的受管理渔业相比——个体理性受机构的约束，防止了悲剧。
When to use / not use: Use collective action logic to predict group formation and maintenance. Large diffuse groups (consumers, taxpayers, pedestrians) struggle to organize unless selective incentives help. Small concentrated groups (industry, wealthy donors, professional elites) organize more easily. Public goods problems explain why some groups produce more activism than others (concentrated benefits and diffuse costs → more organization). For commons problems, explain what institutions exist to prevent depletion and whether those institutions function.
何时用/不用：使用集体行动逻辑来预测群体形成和维持。大而分散的群体（消费者、纳税人、行人）在没有选择性激励的帮助下难以组织。小的集中群体（工业、富有的捐赠者、专业精英）更容易组织。公共商品问题解释了为什么某些群体产生比其他群体更多的激动（集中的好处和分散的成本→更多组织）。对于公地问题，解释存在什么机构来防止枯竭，以及这些机构是否发挥作用。
Social science example: Elinor Ostrom's research on the commons (she won the Nobel Prize for this) showed that communities often develop self-governance solutions without top-down regulation or privatization. Swiss alpine meadows, Japanese forests, Maine lobster fisheries, and Philippine irrigation systems all maintained common pool resources through local institutions. Communities created monitoring systems, punishment for cheating, conflict resolution mechanisms, and governance rules adjusted to local conditions. This suggests Hardin's "tragedy of the commons" (population growth must crash, depletion inevitable) was pessimistic—communities can govern commons successfully when institutions are well-designed.
社会科学例子：Elinor Ostrom关于公地的研究（她因此获得了诺贝尔奖）表明社区往往发展自我管理解决方案，无需自上而下的监管或私有化。瑞士高山草地、日本森林、缅因州龙虾渔业和菲律宾灌溉系统都通过当地机构维持了共同池资源。社区创建了监测系统、对作弊的惩罚、冲突解决机制和针对当地条件调整的治理规则。这表明Hardin的"公地悲剧"（人口增长必须崩溃，枯竭不可避免）是悲观的——当机构设计得很好时，社区可以成功地管理公地。

Solutions: Institutions, Monitoring, and Punishment

解决方案：机构、监测与惩罚

Several institutional solutions to collective action problems exist. Coercion: governments mandate participation (taxes for public goods, military conscription) and punish defection. Markets: privatize the good so owners internalize consequences of depletion (private fishing rights create incentive to sustain stocks). Selective incentives: bundle private goods with public goods (union provides insurance, environmental group provides network). Repeated interaction with reputation: if you'll interact with the same people repeatedly, free-riding becomes costly—you develop reputation for untrustworthiness. Ostrom's synthesis: communities develop institutions combining monitoring (who's complying?), graduated sanctions (first violation gets warning, repeated violations get punished), and conflict resolution (disputes mediated locally). The key insight: collective action is possible without external enforcement if group members can observe each other and sanction defection. This requires small groups (large groups make monitoring hard), repeated interaction, and shared norms about fairness and obligation.

对集体行动问题存在多个制度解决方案。强制：政府强制参与（公共商品税、军事征兵）并惩罚背叛。市场：将商品私有化，使所有者内化枯竭的后果（私有捕鱼权创建维持库存的激励）。选择性激励：将私人商品与公共商品捆绑（工会提供保险，环保组织提供网络）。重复互动与声誉：如果你会与同一批人反复互动，搭便车变成有成本的——你发展出不可信任的声誉。Ostrom的综合：社区发展结合监测（谁在遵守？）、分级制裁（首次违反获得警告、重复违反获得惩罚）和冲突解决（争议本地调解）的机构。关键见解：如果群体成员可以相互观察并制裁背叛，集体行动在没有外部执法的情况下是可能的。这需要小群体（大群体使监测困难）、重复互动以及关于公平和义务的共享规范。

Analogy: A company cafeteria with an honor system (pay what you want) vs. one with a cashier (mandatory payment). The honor system works only if people interact repeatedly (you'll see them again), the amount is small enough that theft isn't tempting, and social norms support paying. A cashier is needed when strangers meet once and large sums are involved—external enforcement substitutes for reputation.
类比：一个有诚实系统的公司食堂（随意支付）与有收银员的食堂（强制支付）。诚实系统仅在人们反复互动（你会再看到他们）、金额足够小以至于盗窃不诱人，以及社会规范支持支付的情况下起作用。当陌生人一次性相遇且涉及大量金额时，需要收银员——外部执行替代声誉。
When to use / not use: Use coercion when free-riding costs are high and monitoring is hard (national defense, public health, law and order). Use markets when the good can be privatized and excludability is feasible. Use selective incentives when you can tie private benefits to group membership. Design institutions around feasible monitoring—large anonymous groups require more external enforcement than small tight-knit groups. Account for the role of social norms and trust—institutional design interacts with culture and history.
何时用/不用：当搭便车成本高且监测困难时使用强制（国防、公共卫生、法律秩序）。当商品可以私有化且排斥是可行的时使用市场。当你可以将私人福利与群体成员身份关联时使用选择性激励。围绕可行的监测设计机构——大的匿名群体比小的紧密群体需要更多的外部执行。考虑社会规范和信任的作用——机构设计与文化和历史相互作用。
Social science example: Climate change negotiations involve repeated interaction (annual meetings, long-term agreements) but weak monitoring (how do we verify emissions reductions?) and distributed costs/benefits (costs are local, benefits are global and delayed). Rational choice predicts weak cooperation, and empirically, climate agreements have been difficult to reach and enforce. Carbon markets attempt to privatize the good (make emitters pay); international reputation serves as partial enforcement; some coalitions use selective incentives (technology transfer, climate finance). But the fundamental problem remains: incentive to free-ride is high because cost of climate damage is distributed and delayed while cost of abatement is immediate and concentrated.
社会科学例子：气候变化谈判涉及重复互动（年度会议、长期协议），但监测薄弱（我们如何验证排放减少？）以及分布式成本/收益（成本是本地，收益是全球和延迟）。理性选择预测合作薄弱，在经验上，气候协议一直难以达成和执行。碳市场试图将商品私有化（使排放者付款）；国际声誉提供部分执行；某些联盟使用选择性激励（技术转让、气候金融）。但根本问题仍然存在：搭便车的动机很高，因为气候损害的成本分布且延迟，而减少成本立即且集中。

Ostrom's Design Principles for Commons Governance

Ostrom关于公地治理的设计原则

From studying successful commons governance, Ostrom identified design principles for robust institutions. Clearly defined boundaries: who has rights to the resource, who is excluded? Congruence between rules and local conditions: rules must fit the ecological and social reality (stocking rates must match meadow capacity). Collective-choice arrangements: people affected by rules have voice in making and modifying them. Monitoring: members or hired monitors observe use and report violations. Graduated sanctions: minor violations get warnings, serious violations face escalating punishment. Conflict resolution: accessible, local mechanisms to resolve disputes before they escalate. Recognition of rights by outside authorities: local governance works better when higher-level authorities recognize local institutions. Nested enterprises: for larger commons, governance is organized at multiple levels (local management within regional coordination). These principles are not universally optimal—they're contingent on context (group size, resource characteristics, external pressures, cultural norms). But they summarize what makes commons governance work in practice, across diverse settings (fisheries, forests, irrigation, grazing lands).

从研究成功的公地治理，Ostrom确定了强大机构的设计原则。清晰界定的边界：谁拥有资源权利，谁被排除？规则与当地条件之间的一致：规则必须符合生态和社会现实（库存率必须与草地容量匹配）。集体选择安排：受规则影响的人在制定和修改规则中有发言权。监测：成员或受雇的监测人员观察使用并报告违规。分级制裁：轻微违规获得警告、严重违规面临升级处罚。冲突解决：可获得的当地机制，在争议升级之前解决。外部当局对权利的认可：当更高级别的当局认可当地机构时，当地治理效果更好。嵌套企业：对于较大的公地，治理在多个层级上进行组织（地方管理在地区协调内）。这些原则并不普遍最优——它们取决于背景（群体规模、资源特征、外部压力、文化规范）。但它们总结了使公地治理在实践中有效工作的原因，跨越多样化设置（渔业、森林、灌溉、牧场）。

Analogy: Open-source software development (Linux, Apache) is a commons where programmers contribute code without market incentives. Governance works through: clear rules (licensing terms), distributed monitoring (code review), reputation (recognition of contributors), and nested decision-making (maintainers approve major changes). When these principles are violated (as in some abandoned open-source projects), the commons collapses—no one maintains code, incompatibilities grow, users migrate to better-maintained alternatives.
类比：开源软件开发（Linux、Apache）是一个公地，程序员在没有市场激励的情况下贡献代码。治理通过以下方式运作：清晰的规则（许可证条款）、分布式监测（代码审查）、声誉（贡献者的认可）和嵌套决策（维护者批准重大变更）。当这些原则被违反时（如在某些放弃的开源项目中），公地崩溃——没有人维护代码，不兼容性增长，用户迁移到维护更好的替代品。
When to use / not use: Ostrom's principles are useful for designing commons governance institutions. Apply them to: fisheries management, water allocation, shared grazing lands, forestry, community pastures, intellectual property commons. Don't expect all principles to apply equally—prioritize based on context. Don't assume centralized top-down management (government control) is superior—evidence suggests community-based governance with clear boundaries, monitoring, and sanctions often performs better. Use these principles as a checklist for institutional design, not as a universal solution.
何时用/不用：Ostrom的原则对于设计公地治理机构很有用。将它们应用于：渔业管理、水分配、共享牧地、林业、社区草地、知识产权公地。不要期望所有原则均等地适用——根据背景优先排序。不要假设集中的自上而下管理（政府控制）优越——证据表明具有清晰边界、监测和制裁的基于社区的治理通常表现得更好。使用这些原则作为机构设计的检查清单，而不是通用解决方案。
Social science example: The Philippine irrigation systems studied by Ostrom had clear boundaries (defining a community), collective choice (farmers participated in rule-making), monitoring (water masters observed use), and graduated sanctions (fines for water theft). These systems sustained high productivity for centuries. Contrast with centralized government irrigation systems that failed due to: lack of local monitoring, inflexible rules that didn't fit local conditions, and lack of farmer participation in management. Ostrom's analysis showed why decentralized community governance sustained commons use better than centralized management—local knowledge, buy-in, and enforcement.
社会科学例子：Ostrom研究的菲律宾灌溉系统有清晰的边界（界定社区）、集体选择（农民参与规则制定）、监测（水民观察使用）和分级制裁（对盗水罚款）。这些系统几个世纪以来维持了高生产力。与因以下原因失败的集中式政府灌溉系统形成对比：缺乏当地监测、不符合当地条件的僵化规则、以及农民在管理中缺乏参与。Ostrom的分析表明为什么分散社区治理比集中管理更好地维持了公地使用——当地知识、认可和执行。

Limits of Rational Choice: Bounded Rationality and Behavioral Extensions

理性选择的限制：有界理性与行为扩展

The problem: People don't always maximize utility. They vote (even though probability of being decisive is near zero). They experience regret and reverse decisions (violating transitivity). They frame choices differently depending on presentation (should be irrelevant if preferences are fixed). They prefer certain outcomes to gambles with identical expected value. Rational choice provides precise predictions, but these predictions often fail. What happens when you relax the assumption of rationality?

问题：人们并不总是最大化效用。他们投票（尽管成为决定性一票的概率接近零）。他们体验遗憾并改变决定（违反及物性）。他们根据呈现方式以不同方式框架化选择（如果偏好固定，应该是无关的）。他们更喜欢某些结果而不是具有相同预期价值的赌博。理性选择提供精确的预测，但这些预测往往失败。当你放松理性假设时会发生什么？

The Paradox of Voting and Bounded Rationality

投票悖论与有界理性

The paradox of voting is famous: if you calculate the probability that your vote is decisive (probability that an election is tied or comes down to one vote), that probability is infinitesimal. In a race with millions of voters, p(decisive) ≈ 0. The cost of voting (time, effort, opportunity cost) is positive. By rational choice, the expected payoff from voting is negative (benefit × 0 = 0, minus cost > 0). Yet millions vote. Early rational choice theorists (Downs 1957) acknowledged this paradox but assumed voters derived "consumption value" from voting (satisfaction from participating in democracy) beyond expected impact. This is rational if you define preferences broadly, but it feels like cheating—adding preferences post-hoc to make voting rational. Simon's "bounded rationality" offers another approach: people don't maximize—they satisfice. They choose the first option that's "good enough" rather than computing every alternative's utility. This requires less cognitive effort and matches how people actually decide. Satisficing explains why people use heuristics (rules of thumb) instead of computing expected utility: they work well enough, and computing is hard. Limits: this explains the adoption of heuristics but not the specific content of many decisions (why do people vote at all if satisficing doesn't require voting?). Additional explanations: voting is a norm (social pressure to participate), voting provides expressive utility (it's an act of political identity), or voting is motivated by altruism (influencing outcomes for others, not oneself).

投票悖论是著名的：如果你计算你的投票具有决定性的概率（选举平局或归结为一票的概率），该概率是无穷小的。在有数百万选民的比赛中，p（决定性）≈ 0。投票的成本（时间、努力、机会成本）是正的。通过理性选择，投票的预期回报是负的（利益×0 = 0，减去成本>0）。然而数百万投票。早期理性选择理论家（Downs 1957）承认这一悖论，但假设选民从投票中获得了"消费价值"（从参与民主中获得的满足感），超越了预期影响。如果你广泛定义偏好，这是理性的，但感觉像是作弊——事后添加偏好以使投票合理。Simon的"有界理性"提供了另一种方法：人们不最大化——他们满足。他们选择第一个"足够好"的选项，而不是计算每个替代方案的效用。这需要更少的认知努力，并匹配人们实际决定的方式。满足解释了为什么人们使用启发式（经验法则）而不是计算预期效用：它们运作得很好，计算很难。限制：这解释了启发式的采用，但没有解释许多决定的具体内容（如果满足不需要投票，为什么人们要投票？）。额外解释：投票是一个规范（参与的社会压力）、投票提供表达效用（这是政治身份的行为）或投票是由利他主义驱动（为他人影响结果，而不是自己）。

Analogy: Maximizing your dinner choice involves pricing each restaurant, estimating quality, computing utility, and selecting the maximum. Satisficing means choosing the first restaurant that meets minimum standards: decent food, reasonable price, convenient location. You don't search for the absolute best restaurant—you search until you find "good enough" and stop. This explains why people don't spend hours choosing restaurants, even though a different choice might have been better.
类比：最大化你的晚餐选择涉及定价每家餐厅、估计质量、计算效用和选择最大值。满足意味着选择第一家符合最低标准的餐厅：不错的食物、合理的价格、方便的位置。你不会搜索绝对最好的餐厅——你搜索直到找到"足够好"然后停止。这解释了为什么人们不会花几个小时选择餐厅，即使不同的选择可能更好。
When to use / not use: Use bounded rationality when people face complex decisions with incomplete information (where to live, which job to take, how to invest). Use heuristic-and-bias approaches when choices are simple and cognitive effort varies (single product decisions, clear alternatives). For voting, acknowledge the paradox: rational choice predicts low turnout (or reliance on consumption value/altruism), but actual turnout is higher. Consider institutional factors (voting rules, ease of voting, social norms) alongside cognitive limitations.
何时用/不用：当人们面临信息不完全的复杂决定时使用有界理性（住在哪里、接受哪个工作、如何投资）。当选择很简单且认知努力变化时使用启发式和偏差方法（单一产品决定、清晰替代）。对于投票，承认悖论：理性选择预测低投票率（或依赖于消费价值/利他主义），但实际投票率更高。考虑制度因素（投票规则、投票便利性、社会规范）以及认知限制。
Social science example: The Iowa caucuses are a test of bounded rationality. Voters make decisions based on limited information (media coverage, personal networks), in a complex sequential game (voting early affects momentum and later voters' perceptions). Rational choice predicts: early voters should vote for the front-runner (safest bet) or for a dark horse if they believe later voters will converge on that candidate. Empirically, information cascades occur: early unexpected results shift perceptions, later voters pile on to the new front-runner, creating momentum for upsets. This suggests voters use heuristics (follow the crowd, vote for momentum) rather than computing full information equilibrium.
社会科学例子：爱荷华州党团会议是有界理性的测试。选民基于有限信息（媒体报道、个人网络）做出决定，在复杂的序列游戏中（早期投票影响动力和后来的选民感知）。理性选择预测：早期选民应该投票给领先者（最安全的赌注），或者如果他们相信后来的选民会聚集在那个候选人身上，就投票给黑马。在经验上，信息级联发生：早期意外结果改变感知，后来的选民蜂拥到新的领先者，为上升创造动力。这表明选民使用启发式（跟随人群、投票获得动力）而不是计算完整信息均衡。

Prospect Theory and Framing Effects

前景理论与框架效应

Kahneman and Tversky's prospect theory identifies systematic violations of expected utility theory. Loss aversion: people feel losses more sharply than gains (losing $100 hurts more than gaining $100 helps). This violates expected utility which treats payoffs symmetrically. The reflection effect: when choices are framed as losses, people become risk-seeking (choosing a risky option with small probability of avoiding loss); when framed as gains, people become risk-averse. The same choice, presented differently, produces different decisions. Probability weighting: people overweight small probabilities and underweight large ones. A 1% chance of winning $1 million feels much bigger than expected utility suggests; a 99% chance feels less significant than probability indicates. Framing matters enormously: describing a medical procedure's success rate (95% survival) vs. mortality rate (5% death) shifts choices, even though they're identical. These effects suggest that rational choice's assumption of fixed preferences is violated: preferences are constructed on the fly, shaped by how choices are presented. This makes prediction harder—the same objective choice problem produces different decisions depending on framing, context, and emotional state.

Kahneman和Tversky的前景理论识别了预期效用理论的系统违规。损失厌恶：人们比收益更敏锐地感受损失（损失100美元比获得100美元帮助更多）。这违反了对称处理回报的预期效用。反射效应：当选择被框架化为损失时，人们变得寻求风险（选择冒险选项以小概率避免损失）；当框架化为收益时，人们变得规避风险。相同的选择，呈现不同，会产生不同的决定。概率加权：人们高估小概率，低估大概率。赢得100万美元的1%机会感觉比预期效用建议的要大得多；99%的机会感觉不如概率指示的那么重要。框架化很重要：描述医疗程序的成功率（95%存活）与死亡率（5%死亡）转变选择，即使它们相同。这些效应表明理性选择关于固定偏好的假设被违反：偏好在现场被构造，由如何呈现选择所塑造。这使预测变得困难——相同的客观选择问题根据框架、背景和情感状态产生不同的决定。

Analogy: A coin flip that gives you $100 vs. the same coin flip that takes away a guaranteed $100 feel very different, even though they have identical expected utility. Framing activates different decision rules: gains activate risk-aversion (protect what you have), losses activate risk-seeking (nothing to lose).
类比：一个给你100美元的硬币翻转与同一个拿走保证的100美元的硬币翻转感觉非常不同，尽管它们有相同的预期效用。框架化激活不同的决策规则：收益激活规避风险（保护你所有），损失激活寻求风险（无所失）。
When to use / not use: Use prospect theory to predict choices in domains where framing is salient (medical decisions, risky bets, policy choices with gain/loss framing). Acknowledge that the same decision, presented as gain vs. loss, produces different choices. Use framing strategically in policy design ("nudges")—default options, loss framing for behavior change, gain framing for acceptance. Don't expect framing to eliminate other factors (cost, habit, norms still matter), but recognize it has substantial effects.
何时用/不用：使用前景理论来预测框架化显著的领域中的选择（医疗决定、有风险的赌注、具有收益/损失框架的政策选择）。承认相同的决定，呈现为收益或损失，会产生不同的选择。在政策设计中战略性地使用框架化（"助推"）——默认选项、行为改变的损失框架、接受的收益框架。不要期望框架化消除其他因素（成本、习惯、规范仍然很重要），但认识到它有实质性影响。
Social science example: Organ donation rates vary dramatically by country—95% in France vs. 12% in Germany, despite similar transplantation infrastructure. The difference: opt-in systems (you must actively sign up) vs. opt-out systems (you're presumed to donate unless you decline). Framing as a choice (gain of being a donor) vs. a default (loss of losing your organs if you don't opt out) produces very different uptake. This is a pure framing effect—the underlying policy is identical, just the frame changes. Similarly, energy conservation appeals phrased as loss (you're wasting money) vs. gain (you'll save money) produce different behavior, with loss frames more effective for behavioral change.
社会科学例子：器官捐赠率因国家而异很大——法国95%对比德国12%，尽管移植基础设施相似。区别：选择加入系统（你必须主动注册）与选择退出系统（如果你不退出，你被假定捐赠）。框架化作为选择（成为捐赠者的收益）对比默认（不退出时失去器官的损失）产生非常不同的吸收。这是纯粹的框架效应——基础政策相同，只是框架改变。类似地，能源节约呼吁被表述为损失（你在浪费金钱）对比收益（你将节省金钱）会产生不同的行为，损失框架对行为改变更有效。

Social Preferences: Altruism, Fairness, and Reciprocity

社会偏好：利他主义、公平性与互惠

Standard rational choice assumes purely selfish preferences: people care only about their own payoff. Ultimatum games disprove this. A proposer offers a split of money ($100) to a responder; if responder rejects, both get zero. Theory predicts: proposer offers minimum acceptable amount (nearly zero), responder accepts because zero is worse. Reality: proposers offer ~40-50%, responders reject unfair offers (10-20%) even at cost to themselves (getting zero). This suggests people care about fairness and punish inequality, independent of material payoffs. Dictator games (responder has no power to reject) show proposers still give 25-40% of the pot—less than ultimatum games but more than zero. This reveals altruism: when power is removed, people still share. Trust games show reciprocity: people are willing to cooperate with trustworthy partners and punish cheaters, even costly. These social preferences have deep roots: they appear across cultures and emerge early in development. They're also domain-specific: people are more altruistic and fair-minded in some contexts (family, community) than others (anonymous market transactions). Incorporating social preferences changes predictions dramatically: conditional cooperation becomes possible (I'll cooperate if you will), altruistic punishment sustains institutions, and fairness concerns motivate behavior beyond material interest.

标准理性选择假设纯粹自私的偏好：人们只关心自己的回报。最后通牒游戏反驳了这一点。提议者提供钱的分割（100美元）给应答者；如果应答者拒绝，两人都得到零。理论预测：提议者提供最小可接受的金额（几乎为零），应答者接受因为零更差。现实：提议者提供~40-50%，应答者拒绝不公平的提议（10-20%），即使对自己有成本（得到零）。这表明人们关心公平并惩罚不平等，独立于物质回报。独裁者游戏（应答者没有拒绝的权力）显示提议者仍然给与罐的25-40%——少于最后通牒游戏但多于零。这揭示了利他主义：当权力被移除时，人们仍然分享。信任游戏显示互惠：人们愿意与值得信任的伙伴合作，惩罚作弊者，即使有成本。这些社会偏好有深厚的根源：它们在各种文化中出现，在发展中早期出现。它们也是特定领域的：人们在某些背景（家庭、社区）中比其他（匿名市场交易）更利他和公平。结合社会偏好会改变预测：条件合作变成可能（如果你会，我会），利他主义惩罚维持机构，公平性关注激励行为超越物质利益。

Analogy: A roommate using your food without asking can respond to different enforcement mechanisms. Selfish rational choice: they'll use your food unless you monitor and punish. Social preferences: they'll feel guilty and ask first, or respect norms about shared property. Reciprocity: if you share with them, they're more likely to share with you. Standard models miss the guilt and norms that actually prevent conflict.
类比：一个舍友不问你就使用你的食物可以对不同的执行机制做出反应。自私理性选择：他们会使用你的食物，除非你监测并惩罚。社会偏好：他们会感到内疚并首先问，或尊重关于共享财产的规范。互惠：如果你与他们分享，他们更可能与你分享。标准模型错过了实际阻止冲突的内疚和规范。
When to use / not use: Incorporate social preferences when studying cooperation, punishment, inequality aversion (redistribution, labor relations, international negotiations). Social preferences are strongest in repeated games and small groups—acknowledge context-dependence. Don't assume everyone has equal social preferences (some people are more selfish, some more altruistic). Use social preferences to explain cooperation in settings where pure self-interest predicts defection.
何时用/不用：在研究合作、惩罚、不平等厌恶（重新分配、劳动关系、国际谈判）时纳入社会偏好。社会偏好在重复游戏和小群体中最强——承认背景依赖。不要假设每个人都有相等的社会偏好（有些人更自私，有些更利他）。使用社会偏好来解释在纯粹自利预测背叛的设置中的合作。
Social science example: Labor negotiations involve social preferences alongside material interests. Workers care about fair wages (not just maximum income) and feel resentment at inequality. Employers who treat workers fairly build loyalty and get higher productivity; those seen as exploitative face strikes and high turnover. Union solidarity reflects reciprocity (you stand with us, we stand with you) more than pure material self-interest. Bargaining that ignores fairness concerns—employers try to impose take-it-or-leave-it offers—often triggers rejection even when objectively reasonable. The fairest bargains are reached through joint problem-solving where both sides feel heard and respected, suggesting social preferences operate alongside economic calculation.
社会科学例子：劳动谈判涉及社会偏好以及物质利益。工人关心公平工资（不仅仅是最大收入）并感到不平等的怨恨。对工人待遇公平的雇主建立忠诚度并获得更高生产率；那些被视为剥削的人面临罢工和高流动率。工会团结反映互惠（你站在我们这一边，我们站在你这一边）比纯粹物质自利更多。忽视公平关注的讨价还价——雇主试图强加于人的提议——通常会引发拒绝，即使在客观上合理。最公平的讨价还价是通过双方都感到被听到和尊重的联合问题解决达到的，这表明社会偏好与经济计算一起运作。

When to Use Rational Choice vs. Behavioral Approaches

何时使用理性选择对比行为方法

Rational choice excels at: institutional analysis (how incentive structures shape behavior), strategic situations with clear payoffs (arms races, voting, entry deterrence), and explanation of aggregate patterns (markets often exhibit equilibrium despite individual irrationality). It's parsimonious—simple models with powerful predictions. Behavioral approaches excel at: individual-level decision-making (when do people use heuristics?), explaining deviations from rational choice predictions, and policy design (nudges exploit behavioral biases). The practical approach: start with rational choice for institutional design and strategic analysis. When predictions fail (people don't behave as theory predicts), add behavioral mechanisms: cognitive limitations, framing effects, social preferences. Test which approach explains observed behavior. For some questions, rational choice is sufficient and elegant (why do legislators support their party's position? Probably because it maximizes their re-election chances). For others, you need behavioral explanation (why do voters vote if probability of decisiveness is zero?). The integration is productive: rational choice provides the baseline, behavioral economics explains deviations, and together they offer fuller understanding than either alone.

理性选择擅长：制度分析（激励结构如何塑造行为）、具有清晰回报的战略形势（军备竞赛、投票、进入阻止）以及总体模式的解释（市场通常表现均衡，尽管个体不理性）。它是节俭的——简单模型具有强大的预测。行为方法擅长：个体水平的决策制定（人们何时使用启发式？）、解释与理性选择预测的偏差，以及政策设计（助推利用行为偏见）。实际方法：从制度设计和战略分析的理性选择开始。当预测失败时（人们不按理论行为），添加行为机制：认知限制、框架效应、社会偏好。测试哪种方法解释观察到的行为。对于某些问题，理性选择是充分和优雅的（为什么立法者支持他们党的立场？可能是因为它最大化了他们的再选举机会）。对于其他问题，你需要行为解释（为什么选民在决定性概率为零时投票？）。集成是富有成效的：理性选择提供基线，行为经济学解释偏差，两者加起来提供比其中任何一个更完整的理解。

Analogy: Rational choice is like Newtonian physics—powerful, elegant, predicts well in many contexts, but breaks down at extreme speeds/scales. Behavioral economics is like quantum mechanics—explains what breaks, but more complex and context-dependent. You use Newtonian physics for everyday engineering, quantum mechanics for subatomic particles. You use rational choice for institutional analysis, behavioral analysis for individual decisions.
类比：理性选择就像牛顿物理学——强大、优雅、在许多背景中预测良好，但在极端速度/规模上破裂。行为经济学就像量子力学——解释什么破裂，但更复杂和背景相关。你对日常工程使用牛顿物理学，对亚原子粒子使用量子力学。你对制度分析使用理性选择，对个体决定使用行为分析。
When to use / not use: Use rational choice: explaining aggregate institutional outcomes, analyzing strategic games, designing policy incentives. Use behavioral: explaining individual deviation from predictions, designing default options and choice architecture, studying how context affects decisions. Don't abandon rational choice because of behavioral anomalies—anomalies are small relative to behavior rational choice predicts. Don't assume behavioral findings are universal—many effects depend on context (culture, group size, experience).
何时用/不用：使用理性选择：解释总体制度成果、分析战略游戏、设计政策激励。使用行为：解释个体与预测的偏差、设计默认选项和选择架构、研究背景如何影响决定。不要因为行为异常而放弃理性选择——相对于理性选择预测的行为，异常很小。不要假设行为发现是普遍的——许多效应取决于背景（文化、群体规模、经验）。
Social science example: Behavioral insights in policy design have used framing effects (loss/gain), defaults (opt-out vs. opt-in), and social preferences (telling people what others do). The UK Behavioural Insights Team used these to increase tax compliance (social proof messages), increase organ donation (opt-out default), and improve energy conservation (normative comparisons). These interventions work because they exploit behavioral patterns, not because rational choice incentives changed. Yet rational choice still explains the baseline behavior—without understanding the incentives (why people pay taxes, donate organs), behavioral interventions would have no guidance.
社会科学例子：政策设计中的行为见解使用了框架效应（损失/收益）、默认（选择退出与选择加入）和社会偏好（告诉人们其他人做什么）。英国行为见解小组使用这些来增加税收遵从（社会证明消息）、增加器官捐赠（选择退出默认）和改善能源节约（规范比较）。这些干预有效是因为它们利用了行为模式，而不是因为理性选择激励改变了。然而理性选择仍然解释了基线行为——如果不理解激励（人们为什么缴税、捐赠器官），行为干预就没有指导。
