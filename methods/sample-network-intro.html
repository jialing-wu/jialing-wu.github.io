Why Study Relationships, Not Just Individuals?

为什么要研究关系，而不只是个体？

Most methods treat observations as independent rows. But many social phenomena are relational — who asks whom for study help reveals hidden learning communities that GPA alone cannot explain. Network analysis shifts the question from "what are this person's attributes?" to "what is their position in a web of relationships?"

大多数方法把观测当作独立的行。但许多社会现象是关系性的——谁找谁请教功课揭示了 GPA 无法解释的隐性学习社群。网络分析把问题从"这个人有什么属性？"转变为"这个人在关系网中处于什么位置？"

Traditional Data
传统数据
Each row = one person, independent
每行 = 一个人，互相独立
Network Data
网络数据
Relationships create structure
关系创造结构
Think of it this way: A resume tells you about a person. A phone log tells you about their world.
换个方式想：简历告诉你一个人。通话记录告诉你他的世界。

The Language of Networks

网络的语言

A network has two building blocks: nodes (actors) and edges (relationships). Mathematically, a network is a graph G = (V, E) where V is the vertex set and E the edge set. Everything else builds on these two. Three key distinctions:

网络有两个基本构件：节点（行动者）和边（关系）。数学上，网络是一个图 G = (V, E)，其中 V 是顶点集，E 是边集。其他一切都建立在这两者之上。三个关键区分：

Undirected
无向
Symmetric adjacency matrix (Aij = Aji). E.g. co-authorship.
对称邻接矩阵（Aij = Aji）。如：合著关系。
Directed
有向
Asymmetric (Aij ≠ Aji). E.g. Twitter follows, citations.
非对称（Aij ≠ Aji）。如：关注、引用。
Weighted
加权
Aij ∈ ℝ (continuous). E.g. trade volume between countries.
Aij ∈ ℝ（连续值）。如：国家间贸易量。

Finding Groups: Community Detection

发现群体：社区检测

Real networks are cliquish — dense within groups, sparse between them. Community detection algorithms discover these groups automatically from connections alone, no labels needed.

真实网络是小团体的——组内密集、组间稀疏。社区检测算法仅从连接数据就能自动发现这些群体，不需要标签。

Dense within groups, sparse between — the dashed line is the only bridge
组内密集、组间稀疏——虚线是唯一的桥梁

Intuition: find groups where members are more connected to each other than to outsiders — like identifying lunch tables in a high school cafeteria. The algorithm doesn't need labels; it discovers structure purely from the pattern of connections. This is powerful because the groups it finds often reveal real social divisions that surveys miss.

直觉：找到成员之间联系比对外联系更紧密的群体——就像识别高中食堂里的午餐桌。算法不需要标签，纯粹从连接模式中发现结构。这很有用，因为它找到的群体常常揭示问卷遗漏的真实社会划分。

From Description to Inference

从描述到推断

Network methods fall into two layers. Descriptive methods tell you what the network looks like; inferential models test why it looks that way.

网络方法分两层。描述性方法告诉你网络长什么样；推断模型检验它为什么长这样。

Descriptive: What Does the Network Look Like?

描述层：网络长什么样？

01
Centrality Measures
中心性指标
Who is important? Degree, betweenness, eigenvector — each captures a different structural role. Covered in Slide 3.
谁重要？度、中介、特征向量——各自捕捉不同的结构角色。见第 3 页。
02
Community Detection
社区检测
Where are the clusters? Algorithms like Louvain optimize modularity to find dense subgroups automatically. Fast, scalable, widely used. Covered in Slide 4.
聚类在哪？Louvain 等算法优化模块性，自动发现密集子群。快速、可扩展、应用广泛。见第 4 页。

Inferential: Why Does the Network Look This Way?

推断层：网络为什么长这样？

Q
QAP
QAP
Do two networks correlate? Permutation-based test. E.g. do students who study together also hang out socially? Shuffle one matrix thousands of times and check if the real correlation is unusually strong.
两个网络是否相关？基于置换的检验。例如：一起学习的学生是否也在课外社交？把一个矩阵洗牌上千次，看真实相关是否异常强。
E
ERGM
ERGM
What drives tie formation? E.g. do students seek help from same-major peers (homophily), or from high-GPA classmates (popularity)? Like logistic regression for networks. Today's focus.
什么驱动关系形成？例如：学生倾向找同专业的人请教（同质性），还是找 GPA 高的人（人气效应）？相当于网络的逻辑回归。今天的重点。
L
Latent Space
潜在空间模型
Embed nodes in a latent space where proximity predicts ties. E.g. map a classroom's friendship network into 2D — students close in the space are likely friends, revealing cliques invisible in raw data.
将节点嵌入潜在空间，距离近的更可能有关系。例如：把一个班级的友谊网络映射到二维空间——空间中靠近的学生更可能是朋友，揭示原始数据中看不到的小圈子。
Today we focus on ERGM — our paper (Köhler et al. 2024) uses it to ask: what drives advice-seeking ties in an organization? Part 2 walks through this step by step.
今天我们聚焦 ERGM——我们要拆解的论文（Köhler et al. 2024）用它来问：什么驱动了组织中的求助关系？第二部分逐步拆解。

Paper Walkthrough: Expertise & Advice Networks

论文拆解：专业知识与建议网络

Köhler, Rausch, Biemann & Büchsenschuss (2024). "Expertise and specialization in organizations: a social network analysis." European Journal of Work and Organizational Psychology, 34(2), 282–297. Open Access

Köhler, Rausch, Biemann & Büchsenschuss (2024). "组织中的专业知识与专业化：一项社会网络分析。" European Journal of Work and Organizational Psychology, 34(2), 282–297. 开放获取

Background: Organizations depend on employees' expertise and specialization to drive innovation and knowledge sharing. But prior research left three gaps: (1) Are you sought for advice because you are truly skilled in that domain, or simply because you are "well-known"? 背景：组织依赖员工的专业知识（expertise）和专业化（specialization）来推动创新和知识共享。但以往研究有三个空白：（1）被人请教是因为你在那个技能领域真的强，还是只要你"出名"就什么都有人来问？（2）什么因素决定了一条求助关系的形成？（3）现实中真正的"专家"和"专才"的技能画像，和理论上描述的一样吗？

Research Question & Core Concepts

研究问题与核心概念

In an organization, who do you turn to when you hit a work problem? Is it the person who is truly the most skilled — or simply the person who is already well-known? This paper asks: What drives the formation of advice-seeking ties in an organizational network? Specifically, do expertise and specialization predict who gets asked, or do structural effects (reciprocity, homophily) dominate?

在组织中，遇到工作问题你会找谁？是真正技能最强的人——还是已经很出名的人？这篇论文问的是：什么驱动了组织建议网络中关系的形成？具体来说，是专业知识和专业化预测谁被咨询，还是结构效应（互惠性、同质性）占主导？

E
Expert (Expertise)
专家（Expertise）
Sustained superior performance relative to peers in a domain. Both breadth and depth of skills are high, developed through long-term accumulation. Network prediction: central position — sought by many.
在某个领域相对于同行有持续卓越的表现。技能广度和深度都高，通过长期积累发展而来。网络预测：处于中心位置——被很多人请教。
S
Specialist (Specialization)
专才（Specialization）
Mid-to-high competence on tasks others don't do — arising from division of labor, not necessarily long-term accumulation. Two forms: sole-responsible (e.g. the only lawyer) or task-specific within a shared domain. Network prediction: peripheral — only those needing that task come to you.
对别人不做的任务有中高水平的能力——来自分工，不一定需要长期积累。两种形态：唯一负责人（如公司唯一的律师）或共同领域内承担特定任务。网络预测：处于边缘——只有需要那几项任务的人才来找你。
Why network analysis? Advice-seeking is inherently relational (who asks whom) and non-independent (if A asks B, B is more likely to ask A back). Standard regression ignores this interdependence — ERGM is designed for it.
为什么用网络分析？建议寻求本质上是关系性的（谁问谁）且非独立的（A 问了 B，B 更可能反过来问 A）。标准回归忽略了这种相互依赖——ERGM 就是为此设计的。

方法：一篇论文中的三个研究

STUDY 1

STUDY 2
ERGM
ERGM

STUDY 3
Skill Profiles
技能画像
Study 1 — Spearman correlations: Is a person's in-degree centrality in one skill domain correlated with their in-degree in other domains, or with their actual skill levels? Tests whether a "halo effect" exists.
Study 2 — ERGM (Exponential Random Graph Model): Models the probability of the entire observed network as P(Y = y) ∝ exp(θT · g(y)). Like logistic regression for networks — simultaneously controls for structural effects (reciprocity, brokerage) and node attributes (expertise, specialization, skill levels, tenure, leadership). Built in 3 steps: Model 1 (edges + reciprocity) → Model 2 (+ endogenous structure) → Model 3 (+ node attributes). Software: R ergm (Statnet).
Study 3 — Optimal Matching: Computes the "distance" between each person's actual skill profile and theoretically defined expert/specialist profiles. Then correlates these distances with self-assessed expertise/specialization scales and network centrality.
研究一 — Spearman 相关：一个人在某个技能领域的入度中心性，与他在其他领域的入度、以及实际技能水平相关吗？检验是否存在"光环效应"。
研究二 — ERGM（指数随机图模型）：将整个观测网络的概率建模为 P(Y = y) ∝ exp(θT · g(y))。相当于网络的逻辑回归——同时控制结构效应（互惠、中介）和节点属性（专业知识、专业化、技能水平、任期、领导责任）。分 3 步构建：模型 1（边 + 互惠性）→ 模型 2（+ 内生结构）→ 模型 3（+ 节点属性）。软件：R ergm（Statnet）。
研究三 — 最优匹配分析：计算每个人的实际技能画像与理论上专家/专才画像的"距离"。然后将这些距离与自评专家/专才量表及网络中心度做相关。
Why this combination? Study 1 reveals the pattern (halo effect); Study 2 tests causal drivers while controlling for network structure; Study 3 validates whether theoretical categories match reality.
为什么用这个组合？研究一揭示模式（光环效应）；研究二在控制网络结构的同时检验驱动因素；研究三验证理论分类是否与现实匹配。

Study 1: The Halo Effect

研究一：光环效应

The core finding: correlations between in-degree centrality across the three skill domains are stronger than correlations between in-degree and actual skill levels within each domain. In plain language: if you're asked for advice on core insurance skills, you're also asked about software and social skills — regardless of your actual ability in those areas.

核心发现：不同技能领域的入度中心性之间的相关性，强于入度与该领域实际技能水平之内的相关性。通俗来说：一个人在核心技能领域被请教多，在软件技能和社交技能领域也会被请教多——不管他在那些领域实际能力如何。

What this means: Being sought for advice reflects overall reputation more than domain-specific competence. People go to "known helpers" regardless of topic. Possible mechanisms: high-visibility employees are more accessible, more willing to help, or colleagues simply don't know each other's specific strengths well enough to discriminate.
这意味着：被请教更多反映的是整体声誉，而非特定领域的能力。人们倾向于找"知名帮手"，不看具体话题。可能的机制：高可见度员工更容易被接触、更愿意帮忙，或者同事们根本不太清楚彼此具体擅长什么。

Contributions & Limitations

理论贡献与局限性

Key theoretical contribution: This paper integrates two previously separate classification frameworks — expert/non-expert and specialist/generalist — and proposes distinguishing four types within the same domain: non-expert, non-specialist, expert, and specialist. The use of ERGM allows simultaneous control of network-endogenous factors, making the analysis more rigorous than simple correlations.

主要理论贡献：这篇文章把专家/非专家和专才/通才这两套以往各说各话的分类框架整合起来，提出在同一个领域内应同时区分四类人：非专家、非专才、专家、专才。ERGM 的使用让研究能同时控制网络结构内生因素，比单纯做相关分析更严谨。

Limitations: All skills are self-assessed (common method variance risk). Each person could name at most 5 contacts, potentially missing important ties. Ego-network data used to infer the complete network introduces some error. Physical proximity (e.g. seating arrangements) was not controlled. The cross-sectional design cannot tell us whether expertise causes more ties or vice versa.

局限性：技能全靠自评（共同方法变异风险）。每人最多提名 5 人，可能遗漏重要关系。用自我网络数据推断全网络有一定误差。没有控制座位相邻等物理因素。横截面设计无法告诉我们是专业知识导致更多关系还是反过来。

Extensions to consider: Use a Temporal ERGM (TERGM) to track network evolution over time. Add objective performance data instead of self-assessed skills. Use SAOM (Stochastic Actor-Oriented Models) to disentangle selection from influence. Expand to multiple organizations for comparison.

可以考虑的延伸：使用时序 ERGM (TERGM) 追踪网络随时间的演变。用客观绩效数据替代自评技能。使用SAOM（随机行动者导向模型）区分选择效应和影响效应。扩展到多个组织进行比较。

总结

Getting Started with Network Analysis

开始你的网络分析

Step 1: Identify relational data — who connects to whom? Step 2: Choose the right representation — directed or undirected, weighted or binary. Step 3: Start descriptive — visualize, compute centrality, detect communities. Step 4: Move to inference — ERGM for tie formation, QAP for network correlation, SAOM for longitudinal dynamics.

第一步：识别关系数据——谁和谁相连？第二步：选择合适的表示——有向或无向，加权或二元。第三步：从描述开始——可视化、计算中心性、检测社区。第四步：进入推断——ERGM 研究关系形成，QAP 检验网络相关，SAOM 分析纵向动态。
